Request Access to a SharePoint Site or Teams Team

Summary

SharePoint sites and Microsoft Teams teams are the primary places Robotix IT employees collaborate on documents and projects. Access is controlled by site/team owners. You can request access through the built-in SharePoint request flow, through Teams, or by submitting a ticket to the Service Desk — this article walks through all three paths.

Before You Begin

  • Know the name or URL of the SharePoint site or Teams team you need access to. You can often get this from a colleague who already has access.
  • Know the access level you need: Read (view only), Edit (add and modify content), or Full Control (owner-level — typically for project leads only).
  • Teams team membership and the associated SharePoint site are linked — joining a Teams team automatically grants access to its SharePoint document library.
  • Some sites are marked Restricted and require IT admin approval in addition to owner approval. If you receive a "This site is restricted" message, open a ticket with IT.

Steps

Option A — Request Access Directly in SharePoint

Use this if you have the URL of the site and receive an "Access Denied" page.

  1. Navigate to the SharePoint site URL (e.g., https://robotix.sharepoint.com/sites/FinancePlanning).
  2. On the Access Denied page, click Request Access.
  3. In the dialog, type a brief reason for needing access (e.g., "I am joining the Q3 budget review project").
  4. Click Send Request.
  5. The site owner receives an email and can approve or deny from within SharePoint. You receive an email notification either way.
  6. Typical response time: 1–2 business days depending on the site owner's availability.

Option B — Join a Teams Team

Use this if you need access to a Teams channel and its associated files.

  1. Open Microsoft Teams.
  2. In the left sidebar, click Teams.
  3. At the bottom of your teams list, click Join or create a team.
  4. Use the search bar at the top to find the team by name.
  5. On the team tile, click Join Team (for public teams) or Request to Join (for private teams).
  6. For private teams, the team owner is notified and must approve your request. You will get a Teams notification when accepted.

Option C — Submit a Service Desk Ticket

Use this when the site owner is unknown, the team is hidden from search, or you need access for a group of people.

  1. Go to https://help.robotix.com.
  2. Navigate to Service Catalog → Accounts & Access → SharePoint / Teams Access Request.
  3. Fill in: - Site or Team Name / URL - Access Level Needed (Read / Edit / Full Control) - Business Justification - Users Needing Access (can list multiple names)
  4. Submit. IT contacts the site/team owner on your behalf and follows up within 1 business day (P4 SLA).

Checking Your Existing Access

To see all SharePoint sites you have access to: 1. Go to https://robotix.sharepoint.com. 2. Click the waffle menu (grid icon, top-left) → SharePoint. 3. Your Following and Recent sites appear. Click My Sites in the left nav to see all sites where you are a member.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
"Request Access" button not visible on the Access Denied page Site owner has disabled the access request feature Submit a Service Desk ticket (Option C above)
Teams team not appearing in search Team is private and hidden from the directory Ask a team member to add you directly, or submit a Service Desk ticket
Access approved but still getting "Access Denied" Permissions not propagated yet in SharePoint Wait up to 30 minutes; clear browser cache; try again
Approved as team member but cannot see certain channels Channel is restricted to a specific channel team Ask the team owner to add you to the specific private channel
Need to add multiple colleagues to a site at once Cannot do this via self-service Submit a Service Desk ticket listing all users

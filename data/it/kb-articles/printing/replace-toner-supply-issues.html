Replace Toner or Report Printer Supply Issues

Summary

Robotix IT monitors toner levels on all HP and Xerox MFPs through PaperCut and orders replacement supplies proactively. However, if a printer runs out of toner or you notice degraded print quality, this article explains how to replace a toner cartridge yourself or report a supply issue to the Service Desk.

Before You Begin

  • Replacement toner cartridges are stocked in each office in the printer supply cabinet (usually near the copy room or mail station). A label on the cabinet lists which cartridges match which printers.
  • Only use Robotix-approved HP and Xerox OEM cartridges stocked on-site. Using non-approved cartridges may void the printer warranty and cause print quality problems.
  • If the supply cabinet is empty, do not order cartridges yourself — submit a request to the Service Desk and IT will order through the approved vendor.
  • Wear gloves if available — toner powder can stain clothing and skin.

Steps

Check Toner Level Before Replacing

  1. On the printer's touchscreen, tap Device Status or the information/settings icon.
  2. Select Supply Status or Toner Levels.
  3. Each color (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) shows a percentage. A cartridge below 10% should be replaced soon; 0% or "Replace Now" means replace immediately.

Replace a Toner Cartridge (HP MFP)

  1. Open the front door of the HP MFP (pull the handle or press the release button).
  2. Locate the cartridge indicated by the printer's front panel (the color that needs replacement).
  3. Grasp the cartridge handle and pull it straight out toward you.
  4. Remove the new cartridge from its packaging. Gently shake it 5–6 times horizontally to distribute the toner evenly.
  5. Remove the orange protective seal strip by pulling the tab firmly in the direction of the arrow.
  6. Align the cartridge with the color-coded slot guide and slide it firmly into the printer until it clicks.
  7. Close the front door. The printer will calibrate for 30–60 seconds before returning to a ready state.

Replace a Toner Cartridge (Xerox MFP)

  1. On the Xerox touchscreen, tap Device > Supplies to identify which cartridge needs replacement.
  2. Open the front cover panel by pressing the release latch on the left side.
  3. Rotate the used cartridge's orange handle 90° counterclockwise, then pull it out by the handle.
  4. Unbox the new cartridge and remove the protective tape from the drum area.
  5. Insert the new cartridge into the slot, aligning the tabs with the grooves. Rotate the orange handle 90° clockwise to lock it in place.
  6. Close the front cover. The printer will initialize the new cartridge (approximately 45 seconds).

Report a Supply Issue to the Service Desk

If the supply cabinet is empty, a cartridge is the wrong model, or the printer shows a supply error you cannot resolve:

  1. Note the printer's asset tag (e.g., SUM-PR-00412) printed on the label on top of the device.
  2. Note the error message displayed on the printer's front panel.
  3. Submit a ticket at https://help.robotix.com using the Printer Supply Request form, or send an email to servicedesk@robotix.com with: - Office location and printer asset tag - Supply type needed (e.g., Black toner, Cyan drum unit) - Current toner level percentage (if visible)
  4. The Service Desk will arrange delivery or dispatch a technician, typically within one business day (P3).

Troubleshooting

Symptom What to try
Print quality is streaky or faded after new cartridge Reseat the cartridge — open the front door, remove, re-insert, and close. Run a Cleaning Page from the printer's maintenance menu.
Printer says "Invalid Cartridge" Confirm the cartridge matches the model listed on the supply cabinet label. Non-OEM or wrong-model cartridges trigger this error.
Toner spilled during replacement Do not use a vacuum cleaner (toner can damage motors). Use a dry cloth or dry paper towels to blot the spill. Report large spills to Facilities.
Front door won't close after cartridge install The cartridge is not fully seated. Remove it and reinsert until it clicks. Ensure no packaging material remains inside.
Printer still shows low toner after replacement Perform a Supply Reset from the printer's Admin menu, or power-cycle the printer. If the level does not update, submit a ticket.

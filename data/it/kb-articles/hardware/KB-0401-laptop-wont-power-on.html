Laptop Won't Power On

Summary

This article walks through the steps to diagnose and resolve a laptop that will not power on or boot. It covers both the Dell Latitude 7440 (Windows) and MacBook Pro 14" (Apple silicon). Most no-power situations are caused by a drained battery, a faulty connection at the dock, or a frozen firmware state — all of which can be resolved without sending the device in for repair.

Before You Begin

  • Gather your laptop's asset tag (label on the bottom of the device, format SUM-LT-##### or SUM-MB-#####).
  • Try these steps in order. Each step eliminates a likely cause before moving to the next.
  • If the laptop was recently exposed to liquid or physical impact, do not attempt to power it on. Submit a repair request immediately (see KB-0407).

Steps

Dell Latitude 7440 (Windows 11)

  1. Disconnect from the dock and connect power directly. Unplug the laptop from the Dell WD19 docking station. Connect the included 65 W USB-C power adapter directly to the laptop's USB-C port. Wait 2 minutes.
  2. Check the power adapter LED. The adapter's indicator light should be solid white. If it is off or amber, try a different power outlet or swap the adapter with a spare (contact IT for a loaner adapter if needed).
  3. Press and hold the power button for 10 seconds. This performs a force shutdown of any hung firmware state. Release, then press the power button once normally.
  4. Perform a battery reset. If the laptop still does not respond, hold the power button + the D key simultaneously for 3 seconds. The LCD diagnostic LEDs on the side of the unit will cycle — this confirms the main board is receiving power. Release and press the power button once.
  5. Check for POST indicators. On pressing the power button, watch for any LED activity near the power button or camera, and listen for fan spin. If LEDs blink in a pattern but the screen is blank, note the pattern (e.g., 2 amber + 3 white) and reference the Dell Latitude 7440 LED diagnostic guide or contact the service desk with that code.
  6. Try an external display. Connect a Dell monitor via USB-C or HDMI. If an image appears on the external display, the laptop's built-in display may be faulty — submit a repair request (KB-0407).
  7. If none of the above works, the device likely has a hardware fault. Do not attempt further disassembly. Proceed to KB-0407 to request a repair or KB-0408 for a loaner while the device is serviced.

MacBook Pro 14" (Apple silicon)

  1. Disconnect from the dock and connect power directly. Unplug from any USB-C hub or Dell dock. Connect the Apple USB-C or MagSafe cable directly to the MacBook.
  2. Wait 30 seconds, then press the power button once. Apple silicon Macs may take a moment to register a charge when fully drained.
  3. Check charging indicator. If using MagSafe, the LED should glow amber (charging) or green (charged). No light indicates a bad cable or adapter — try a different cable.
  4. Perform a hard reset. Press and hold the power button for 10 seconds until the Mac shuts down (you may hear a click). Wait 5 seconds, then press the power button once.
  5. Start in Safe Boot to rule out software. Press and hold the power button until "Loading startup options" appears. Select your startup disk, hold Shift, and click Continue in Safe Mode. If the Mac boots in Safe Mode, a login item or kernel extension is likely the culprit; contact IT for further analysis.
  6. Check for a kernel panic loop. If the Apple logo appears but the Mac restarts repeatedly, hold the power button to force off, then contact IT — this may indicate a macOS corruption that requires a Jamf re-enrollment wipe.
  7. If none of the above works, escalate to IT. Do not attempt to open the device casing.

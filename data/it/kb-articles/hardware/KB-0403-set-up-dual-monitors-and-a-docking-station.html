Set Up Dual Monitors and a Docking Station

Summary

This article explains how to connect and configure your Dell WD19 docking station with up to two Dell 24" or 27" USB-C monitors. It covers cable connections, display arrangement in Windows 11 and macOS, and common issues such as blank screens or monitors not being detected.

Before You Begin

  • Confirm you have the following equipment (all issued by IT):
  • Dell WD19 docking station with its 130 W power adapter (the large rectangular brick with a barrel connector)
  • One USB-C cable to connect the dock to your laptop (included with the dock)
  • Up to two Dell 24"/27" monitors with their DisplayPort or HDMI cables
  • Your laptop: Dell Latitude 7440 or MacBook Pro 14"
  • The Dell WD19 requires its own power adapter to charge your laptop through the dock. If the adapter is missing, the dock will not function correctly.
  • Do not connect the dock power adapter to a power strip rated under 15 A — use a surge-protected power bar or wall outlet directly.

Steps

1. Connect the Docking Station

  1. Place the Dell WD19 dock on your desk in an accessible location with at least 2 inches of clearance around the vents.
  2. Connect the dock's power adapter barrel connector to the dock's DC-in port on the back. Plug the other end into a wall outlet or power bar. The dock's power LED (front) should turn solid white.
  3. Connect your monitors to the dock: - Monitor 1: Use a DisplayPort cable from the dock's DisplayPort 1 port to the monitor's DisplayPort input. - Monitor 2: Use a DisplayPort cable from the dock's DisplayPort 2 port (or HDMI port if one monitor uses HDMI) to the second monitor.
  4. Power on both monitors.
  5. Connect the USB-C cable from the dock's upstream USB-C port to your laptop. Use the cable labeled "To Laptop" if provided.

2. Verify Detection (Windows 11 — Dell Latitude 7440)

  1. Windows should detect the monitors automatically within 30 seconds. If not, press Windows + P and select Extend to trigger display detection.
  2. Right-click the desktop and select Display settings.
  3. Under Rearrange your displays, you should see three display boxes (laptop screen + 2 monitors). Click Identify to see which number corresponds to each physical screen.
  4. Drag the display boxes to match your physical monitor layout (e.g., laptop in the center, monitors to the left and right).
  5. Select your primary display from the list and check Make this my main display.
  6. Set resolution for each monitor: Dell 24" monitors run best at 1920 × 1200 and Dell 27" monitors at 2560 × 1440. Select each display and set resolution under Display resolution.
  7. Click Keep changes when prompted.

3. Verify Detection (macOS — MacBook Pro 14")

  1. macOS detects monitors automatically when the USB-C cable is connected. If a monitor is blank, go to Apple menu > System Settings > Displays and click Detect Displays in the lower-right corner.
  2. In Displays, you will see thumbnails for each connected screen. Drag them to match your physical layout.
  3. To set a primary display (where the menu bar lives), drag the white menu bar strip from one thumbnail to another.
  4. Click Use as Extended Display for each external monitor if prompted to choose between mirroring and extending.
  5. Set resolution: click each display thumbnail, select More Space or click the specific resolution matching the monitor's native resolution (1920 × 1200 for 24", 2560 × 1440 for 27").

4. Connect Peripherals Through the Dock

  1. Plug your Logitech keyboard and mouse USB receivers (or cable) into the USB-A ports on the front or back of the dock.
  2. Connect your Jabra or Logitech headset to the dock's USB port or 3.5 mm audio jack.
  3. Connect a network cable to the dock's RJ-45 (Ethernet) port if you are in an office. This will be your primary network connection — faster and more reliable than Wi-Fi.

Troubleshooting

Issue Action
One or both monitors show no signal Reseat all DisplayPort/HDMI cables at both ends. Try pressing Windows + Ctrl + Shift + B (Windows) to reset the graphics driver. On Mac, go to System Settings > Displays > Detect Displays.
Only one monitor detected, not two Ensure both cables are fully seated in the dock. Check that each monitor is powered on and set to the correct input (DisplayPort or HDMI).
Laptop is not charging through the dock Verify the dock's 130 W power adapter is connected and the dock's power LED is white. Try the USB-C cable directly from the laptop to the adapter as a test.
Monitors flicker or go blank intermittently Replace the DisplayPort cable — intermittent flicker is almost always a faulty cable. Contact IT for a replacement.
macOS only mirrors instead of extending Go to System Settings > Displays, select the external display thumbnail, and change the drop-down from "Mirror" to "Extend Display."
External display resolution looks blurry Open Display settings and set the resolution to the monitor's native resolution (see Step 11 / Step 10 above).
Keyboard/mouse not working through the dock Plug the peripheral directly into the laptop USB port to confirm it works, then reconnect to the dock. Try a different USB port on the dock.

You Can't Reach an Internal Site or Application

Summary

If you can browse the public internet but cannot reach an internal Robotix IT resource — such as an intranet site, SharePoint library, a business application hosted on *.robotix.com or a private IP address, or a shared network drive — the most likely cause is that GlobalProtect VPN is not connected, the VPN tunnel has dropped, or a DNS issue is preventing name resolution. This article walks through diagnosing and fixing the problem.

Before You Begin

  • Know the address of the resource you are trying to reach (e.g., https://intranet.robotix.com, \\fileserver01\shared, or the app URL your manager provided).
  • Determine whether you are in the office (on Robotix-Corp Wi-Fi or wired) or working remotely.
  • If you are in the office and still cannot reach the resource, skip to Step 3 — the issue may be permissions or a service outage rather than VPN.

Steps

Step 1 — Confirm GlobalProtect is connected (remote workers)

If you are not on the Robotix-Corp office network, GlobalProtect VPN must be active to reach internal resources.

  1. Look at the GlobalProtect icon in the system tray (Windows) or menu bar (Mac). - Green globe = Connected. Proceed to Step 2. - Grey / outline globe = Not connected. Click the icon and select Connect, then retry the resource.
  2. If GlobalProtect fails to connect, see KB-0304.

Step 2 — Confirm the VPN tunnel is routing internal traffic

A VPN can show "Connected" but still fail to route traffic properly if the tunnel dropped silently.

  1. Click the GlobalProtect icon.
  2. Click Disconnect, wait 10 seconds, then click Connect again.
  3. Approve the Okta Verify MFA push.
  4. Once reconnected, retry the internal resource.

Step 3 — Test with the IP address instead of the hostname

This isolates whether the issue is DNS (name resolution) or true network access.

  1. Contact the Service Desk or your IT contact to obtain the IP address of the resource.
  2. Try navigating to https://<IP address> (or connecting via IP for file shares). - If it works by IP but not by name → DNS issue. See KB-0308. - If it does not work by IP either → Network/permissions issue. Proceed to Step 4.

Step 4 — Clear your browser cache and try a different browser

Cached incorrect DNS entries or TLS errors can block access.

Windows / macOS — Chrome: 1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete (Windows) or Cmd + Shift + Delete (Mac). 2. Set the time range to All time, check Cached images and files and Cookies and other site data, and click Clear data. 3. Close and reopen the browser, then retry the URL.

Also try in Microsoft Edge or Safari to rule out a browser-specific issue.

Step 5 — Flush DNS cache

Stale DNS entries can point to old or invalid IP addresses.

Windows 11: 1. Open Windows Terminal or Command Prompt as Administrator. 2. Run: ipconfig /flushdns 3. You should see: "Successfully flushed the DNS Resolver Cache." 4. Run: ipconfig /registerdns 5. Retry the resource.

macOS: 1. Open Terminal. 2. Run: sudo dscacheutil -flushcache; sudo killall -HUP mDNSResponder 3. Enter your password and press Return. 4. Retry the resource.

Step 6 — Check if the service is down

The resource may be temporarily unavailable for everyone, not just you.

  1. Open Slack and check the #it-announcements or #it-help channel for any active maintenance or outage notices.
  2. You can also open https://help.robotix.com, sign in, and check the Service Status dashboard.
  3. If an outage is posted, wait for the resolution update and do not open a duplicate ticket.

Step 7 — Verify you have permission to access the resource

Some internal resources are restricted to specific teams, roles, or security groups.

  • Confirm with your manager that you should have access.
  • If access was recently requested, it may still be pending provisioning (allow up to one business day for new REQ tickets).
  • If you believe access was removed in error, ask your manager to submit an access request at https://help.robotix.com.

Step 8 — Check your network connection on-site (office users)

If you are at the office and connected to Robotix-Corp Wi-Fi or a wired Ethernet port but still cannot reach the resource:

  1. Try disconnecting from Wi-Fi and reconnecting to Robotix-Corp (see KB-0300).
  2. Try a different Ethernet port at your desk.
  3. Restart your network adapter: - Windows: Settings > Network & internet > Advanced network settings > click your adapter > Disable, then Enable. - macOS: System Settings > Wi-Fi > toggle off, wait 5 seconds, toggle on.

Troubleshooting Quick Reference

Symptom Likely Cause First Action
Works on public internet, not internal apps VPN not connected Connect GlobalProtect (KB-0302 / KB-0303)
VPN connected but app times out Tunnel dropped silently Disconnect and reconnect GlobalProtect
Works by IP, not by name DNS issue Flush DNS cache (Step 5); see KB-0308
Error: "Access Denied" or HTTP 403 Permissions Ask manager to submit an access request
Resource unreachable for whole team Service outage Check #it-announcements; open a P2 ticket
File share \\server\share not mapping VPN or DNS Ensure VPN connected; use full FQDN \\fileserver01.robotix.com\share

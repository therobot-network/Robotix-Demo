Internet Is Slow at the Office

Summary

Slow internet at the office can stem from a congested wireless access point, a device connected to the wrong Wi-Fi band, a background application consuming bandwidth, or a temporary network issue. This article helps you identify the cause and restore normal performance. If the problem affects your entire team or floor, it may be a network infrastructure issue — report it to the Service Desk so engineering can investigate.

Before You Begin

  • Confirm you are connected to Robotix-Corp (not Robotix-Guest). Robotix-Guest is a lower-priority guest network and is not intended for production work.
  • Run a quick speed test to establish a baseline before making changes: visit https://speed.cloudflare.com from your device.
  • Expected speeds on Robotix-Corp: ≥ 50 Mbps down / ≥ 20 Mbps up when near an access point.
  • Note whether the slowness is only in the browser, in specific apps (Teams video, OneDrive sync), or across everything.

Steps

Step 1 — Check if the problem is device-specific or network-wide

  1. Ask a colleague nearby: is their connection also slow? - If multiple people on the same floor are slow → likely a network issue. Skip to Step 7 and open a ticket. - If only your device is slow → proceed through the steps below.

Step 2 — Move closer to a wireless access point

Wi-Fi performance degrades significantly with distance and physical obstructions (walls, cubicle partitions, glass).

  1. Note your current speed at https://speed.cloudflare.com.
  2. Move to within 15–20 feet of a known access point (APs are usually ceiling-mounted, about every 30–40 feet in open areas).
  3. Run the speed test again and compare.
  4. If speed improves significantly near the AP, your original desk location has poor signal. Submit a REQ ticket at https://help.robotix.com requesting an AP coverage review for that area.

Step 3 — Disconnect and reconnect to Robotix-Corp

Refreshing the Wi-Fi connection forces your device to re-associate to the nearest and least-congested access point.

Windows 11: 1. Click the Wi-Fi icon in the taskbar. 2. Click Robotix-Corp > Disconnect. 3. Wait 10 seconds, then click Robotix-Corp > Connect. 4. Sign in with your Okta credentials if prompted.

macOS: 1. Hold Option and click the Wi-Fi menu in the menu bar. 2. Click Disconnect from Robotix-Corp. 3. Wait 10 seconds, then reconnect to Robotix-Corp.

Step 4 — Forget the network and rejoin (forces fresh association)

Windows 11: 1. Go to Settings > Network & internet > Wi-Fi > Manage known networks. 2. Click Robotix-Corp > Forget. 3. Reconnect from the Wi-Fi taskbar icon (enter your Okta credentials when prompted).

macOS: 1. Open System Settings > Wi-Fi. 2. Click the i next to Robotix-Corp > Forget This Network. 3. Reconnect (System Settings > Wi-Fi > Robotix-Corp) and enter your Okta credentials.

Step 5 — Close bandwidth-heavy background applications

Applications running in the background can saturate your connection even when you are not actively using them.

Common bandwidth consumers to check and pause: - OneDrive / SharePoint sync — Click the OneDrive icon in the system tray or menu bar > Pause syncing > 2 hours. - Teams background updates — Quit Teams if you are not in a call. - Windows Update / Intune policies — Check Settings > Windows Update for an active download. Updates may be downloading automatically during business hours. - Zoom or Teams video calls — Lower your camera resolution: in Teams, go to Settings > Devices > Camera and reduce resolution to 720p. - Streaming media — Close any browser tabs playing video.

Step 6 — Check for interference from personal hotspots

If you or a colleague is broadcasting a personal mobile hotspot on the same frequency range as Robotix-Corp, it can cause interference.

  1. Ensure your phone's personal hotspot is turned off when at the office.
  2. Ask nearby colleagues to do the same.

Step 7 — Report a floor or building-wide issue

If multiple people are experiencing slow speeds or you have ruled out all device-level causes:

  1. Run the speed test one more time and screenshot the result.
  2. Open a ticket at https://help.robotix.com with: - Your floor and office location (e.g., "Denver HQ, 3rd floor, east wing") - The speed test result and URL (speed.cloudflare.com) - Number of affected people (if known) - Time the slowness started
  3. Mark the ticket P2 – High if the entire floor is affected. The L3 network team will investigate.

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
Slow on Wi-Fi, fast on Ethernet Wi-Fi congestion or signal Move closer to AP; reconnect (Steps 2–4)
Slow for everyone on the floor AP or uplink issue Open a P2 ticket (Step 7)
Slow only during video calls Bandwidth cap on Teams/Zoom Lower camera resolution; pause OneDrive sync
Connected to Robotix-Guest by mistake Wrong SSID Disconnect and join Robotix-Corp (KB-0300)
Speed test shows < 5 Mbps from near the AP AP fault Contact Service Desk — possible hardware issue
Slow after laptop woke from sleep Stale Wi-Fi association Disconnect and reconnect to Robotix-Corp (Step 3)

Secure Your Home Office and Wi-Fi

Summary

Working from home introduces security risks that do not exist in a corporate office — from unsecured Wi-Fi networks to family members on the same network. This article provides practical steps to harden your home office setup and keep Robotix IT data safe when you work remotely.

Why This Matters

When you work from home, your home router and Wi-Fi network become part of Robotix IT's security perimeter. A poorly configured home network can allow attackers to intercept traffic, access your device, or pivot to company systems. GlobalProtect VPN encrypts all traffic between your device and Robotix IT's network, but your home network itself needs to be reasonably secure first.

Steps

Part 1: Connect to GlobalProtect VPN

Robotix IT requires GlobalProtect VPN for accessing all internal applications and resources from outside the corporate network.

  1. Open the Palo Alto GlobalProtect app on your laptop (search "GlobalProtect" in the Start Menu or Applications folder).
  2. If not already connected, click Connect.
  3. Authenticate using your Okta credentials and approve the Okta Verify MFA push on your phone.
  4. The VPN icon in your taskbar or menu bar should show a green shield when connected.
  5. Keep VPN connected during your entire work session. Disconnect only when you are done with work for the day.

If GlobalProtect does not connect, contact the Service Desk at https://help.robotix.com.

Part 2: Secure Your Home Wi-Fi Router

  1. Change your router's default admin password. Log in to your router's admin panel (usually at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1 — check the label on the back of your router). Change the admin password to a unique, strong password saved in 1Password.
  2. Set your Wi-Fi to WPA3 or WPA2. In your router's wireless settings, ensure the security mode is WPA3-Personal or at minimum WPA2-AES. Avoid WEP or WPA (TKIP) — these are insecure.
  3. Use a strong, unique Wi-Fi passphrase. Generate one in 1Password (20+ characters). Do not use your address, pet name, or a phrase you use elsewhere.
  4. Keep your router firmware up to date. Check your router manufacturer's website or the admin panel for firmware updates. Enable automatic updates if available.
  5. Disable remote management. In the router admin panel, turn off remote management / remote access to the admin panel from the internet.
  6. Disable WPS (Wi-Fi Protected Setup). WPS is a known vulnerability. Disable it in the router's wireless settings.

Part 3: Separate Work and Personal Traffic

If your router supports it, create a separate guest Wi-Fi network for personal devices, smart home devices, and family members:

  1. In your router's admin panel, look for Guest Network or SSID 2.
  2. Enable a second SSID with a different password.
  3. Enable Client Isolation on the guest network so devices on it cannot communicate with each other or with your work laptop.
  4. Connect your Robotix IT laptop only to your primary, secured Wi-Fi network.
  5. Connect personal phones, tablets, smart TVs, and IoT devices to the guest network.

Part 4: Secure Your Physical Work Space

  1. Lock your screen when stepping away — even for a moment. Windows: Win + L. macOS: Ctrl + Cmd + Q.
  2. Use a privacy screen filter on your laptop if you work in shared or public spaces.
  3. Do not leave your laptop unattended in public (cafes, libraries, airports). If you must step away, take your laptop with you or lock it with a physical cable lock.
  4. Position your screen away from windows and shared areas to prevent visual eavesdropping.
  5. Keep your laptop off shared surfaces where family members or visitors might accidentally access it.

Part 5: Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Work Tasks

  • Do not connect your Robotix IT laptop to public Wi-Fi (cafes, hotels, airports) without using GlobalProtect VPN.
  • Even with VPN, avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks (approving payments, accessing HR data, etc.) when possible — use your phone's mobile hotspot instead.
  • If you must use hotel or airport Wi-Fi: connect to GlobalProtect VPN immediately after connecting. Do not accept any unusual certificate warnings.

Troubleshooting / Warning Signs

  • GlobalProtect fails to connect from home: Check that your home internet is working, then retry. If it still fails, contact the Service Desk. Do not access internal resources without VPN.
  • My router admin page asks for "admin / admin" or "admin / password": These are default credentials that attackers know. Change them immediately per Part 2, Step 1.
  • A neighbor or visitor asks for your Wi-Fi password: Give them the guest network password only, not your primary network password.
  • I received a certificate warning when connecting to GlobalProtect: Do not bypass it. Contact the Service Desk — this could indicate a network-level attack (SSL interception).
  • My home internet is down: Use your phone's mobile hotspot. Connect your laptop via USB tethering or Wi-Fi hotspot, then connect GlobalProtect VPN as normal.

What to Do If You Clicked a Suspicious Link or Attachment

Summary

If you clicked a link or opened an attachment from a suspicious email, text, or website, act immediately. Fast action limits the damage. This article walks you through exactly what to do in the first 10 minutes.

Why This Matters

A single click can hand attackers your credentials, install keyloggers or ransomware, or give them remote access to your device — and through your device, to Robotix IT's network. Speed matters: the sooner the SOC is notified, the sooner they can contain the threat before it spreads.

Steps

Step 1 — Stop and disconnect (within the first 2 minutes)

  1. Do not click anything else on the page or in the file. Close the browser tab or document immediately.
  2. If a page asked you to enter your Okta, Microsoft 365, or any other password and you typed it: note this — you will need to report it in Step 3.
  3. Disconnect from the network: - Wi-Fi: Click the Wi-Fi icon in your taskbar (Windows) or menu bar (macOS) and select Disconnect or turn Wi-Fi off. - Wired: Unplug the Ethernet cable from your laptop or dock. - Do not power off the device — this can destroy forensic evidence the SOC needs.

Step 2 — Contact the SOC immediately

  1. From another device (your phone or a colleague's computer), call the Robotix Service Desk: +1 (800) 555-7866 and say "I clicked a suspicious link on my work laptop."
  2. Or email soc@robotix.com from your personal email. Include: - Your name and employee ID (SUM-#####) - Your laptop asset tag (SUM-LT-##### label on the bottom of the device) - The approximate time you clicked the link - What happened after (page loaded, file opened, you entered a password, pop-up appeared, etc.) - The URL or sender address if you can recall it
  3. The SOC is available 24/7 for security incidents.

Step 3 — Change passwords if you entered credentials

If you typed any password after clicking the link, change it immediately from a different, unaffected device:

  1. Go to https://robotix.okta.com from your phone or another computer.
  2. Sign in and navigate to your account settings to change your Okta password.
  3. If you reuse that password anywhere else (you should not — see KB-0705), change it on those sites too.
  4. Tell the SOC which accounts may be compromised when you contact them in Step 2.

Step 4 — Leave your device on and available for the SOC

  1. Keep the device powered on and plugged in (but disconnected from the network).
  2. Do not attempt to run antivirus scans yourself — CrowdStrike Falcon is already monitoring the device, and the SOC will direct next steps.
  3. Do not wipe or factory-reset the device without SOC instruction.
  4. The SOC or a Desktop team member will contact you to perform remote triage or collect the device.

Step 5 — Document what happened

While your memory is fresh, write down:

  • The email sender address and subject line (or the website URL)
  • The time and date
  • Exactly what you clicked (link text, button label, file name)
  • What appeared on screen afterward
  • Whether you entered any information

This helps the SOC investigation and your incident report.

Troubleshooting / Warning Signs

  • Pop-up says your computer is infected and asks you to call a number: This is a tech-support scam. Do not call the number. Close the browser tab (or force-quit the browser). Contact the SOC.
  • Screen locks with a ransom message: This may be ransomware. Do not pay. See KB-0704 immediately.
  • CrowdStrike Falcon shows an alert on screen: Follow the on-screen instructions and contact the SOC. Do not dismiss the alert.
  • Browser redirected to a Microsoft or Google login page: You may have entered credentials into a fake login. Treat this as a credential compromise — complete Step 3 immediately.
  • Nothing seemed to happen after clicking: That does not mean nothing occurred. Malware can install silently. Still contact the SOC.

Report a Lost Laptop or Possible Data Breach

Summary

If your Robotix IT laptop is lost, stolen, or you suspect company data has been exposed or accessed without authorization, you must report it immediately. This article explains what to do, why time matters, and what Robotix IT will do to protect the data on your device.

Why This Matters

Robotix IT laptops may contain Confidential or Restricted data, cached credentials, and access to cloud systems. If a device falls into the wrong hands, a fast response — especially a remote device wipe — can prevent a minor loss from becoming a major breach. Regulatory frameworks that Robotix IT operates under may require breach notification within 72 hours of discovery; your prompt report enables the company to meet that obligation.

Before You Begin

  • All Robotix IT laptops are enrolled in Microsoft Intune (Windows) or Jamf Pro (macOS), which enables remote lock and remote wipe.
  • Windows laptops are encrypted with BitLocker. Mac laptops use FileVault. This means data on the drive is unreadable without authentication — but this does not eliminate the need to report.
  • If you believe a breach is in progress (e.g., you can see unauthorized activity right now), call the SOC immediately: +1 (800) 555-7866. Do not follow this article sequentially — call first.

Steps

Step 1 — Report within 1 hour of discovery

The most important step is speed. Report a lost or stolen device as soon as you realize it is missing — do not wait until you have "looked everywhere" or until you get home. Every hour of delay reduces the effectiveness of a remote wipe.

Contact the SOC immediately through any of these channels: - Phone (fastest): +1 (800) 555-7866 — available 24/7 for security incidents - Email: soc@robotix.com from your phone or another device - Portal: https://help.robotix.com — open a ticket with category "Security Incident – Lost/Stolen Device"

Step 2 — Provide key information

When you contact the SOC or Service Desk, have the following ready:

  • Your name and employee ID (SUM-#####)
  • Device asset tag (on a sticker on the bottom of the laptop: SUM-LT-##### for Windows, SUM-MB-##### for Mac)
  • Device serial number (if known — found in System Settings > About on macOS, or Settings > System > About on Windows)
  • When and where the device was last seen (approximate time and location)
  • How it was lost or stolen (left in a car, bag stolen, left at a coffee shop, etc.)
  • Whether the device was locked when last seen (screen lock / sleep mode active)
  • What data or applications were open when you last used it
  • Whether the device was connected to Wi-Fi at the time of loss

Step 3 — The SOC will initiate a remote lock and wipe

Once the SOC confirms they have your ticket, they will:

  1. Remote lock the device via Intune or Jamf, preventing new logins immediately.
  2. If the device cannot be recovered within a reasonable timeframe, initiate a remote wipe to erase all data.
  3. Suspend your Okta session on the lost device to revoke any active cloud sessions.
  4. Audit access logs to determine whether any data was accessed after the loss.

You do not need to take these steps yourself.

Step 4 — Change your passwords from a different device

As a precaution:

  1. From your phone or another computer, go to https://robotix.okta.com.
  2. Change your Okta password using the guidelines in KB-0705.
  3. Review your Okta account's active sessions and sign out of all sessions except your current one.
  4. If you stored any passwords outside of 1Password (e.g., a password saved in your browser), change those accounts.

Step 5 — File a police report if the device was stolen

If the laptop was stolen (not simply misplaced):

  1. File a police report with your local law enforcement agency as soon as possible.
  2. Request a copy of the report or a case number.
  3. Provide the case number to the SOC and the Service Desk — this is required for insurance purposes and may support the investigation.

Step 6 — Cooperate with the SOC investigation

The SOC may ask you follow-up questions about recent file access, where you traveled, or what applications you had open. Respond promptly and honestly. The investigation is not disciplinary — it is necessary to determine whether notifications to clients, partners, or regulators are required.

Reporting a Suspected Data Breach (Not Device Loss)

If you believe company data has been exposed, accessed, or taken without a device being lost or stolen (for example, you sent a file to the wrong person, found data in a public location, or noticed unauthorized access to a system):

  1. Stop the exposure if you can (delete the email, revoke the sharing link, etc.).
  2. Contact the SOC immediately at soc@robotix.com or +1 (800) 555-7866.
  3. Do not attempt to assess the severity yourself before reporting — that determination belongs to the SOC and Legal team.
  4. Do not notify affected individuals, clients, or partners without explicit direction from Legal and the SOC.

Troubleshooting / Warning Signs

  • I found the device after reporting it lost: Contact the SOC immediately. If a remote wipe was already initiated, the device will need to be re-enrolled. This is a routine process — do not be embarrassed to report a false alarm.
  • Someone else found my laptop and returned it: Do not use the device without contacting the SOC first. Even if it appears untampered, it must be inspected before reconnecting to Robotix IT systems.
  • The laptop is still at a hotel or airport: Contact the hotel or airport lost-and-found and provide details. Do this in parallel with reporting to the SOC — do not delay the SOC report while pursuing recovery.
  • I'm not sure if any sensitive data was on the device: Report anyway. The SOC can review your OneDrive, browser, and sync logs to determine what was accessible.

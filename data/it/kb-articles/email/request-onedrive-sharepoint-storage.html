Request More OneDrive or SharePoint Storage

Summary

Each Robotix IT user is allocated 1 TB of OneDrive for Business storage by default. SharePoint team sites and Microsoft Teams channels share a per-site quota managed by IT. If you are approaching your storage limit or need more space for a project, this article explains how to check your current usage and submit a storage increase request.

Before You Begin

  • Before requesting additional storage, review whether you can free up space by removing old files, downloading and deleting large archives, or moving project files to a SharePoint team site (which has separate quota).
  • OneDrive storage increases are reviewed and approved by the IT Infrastructure team. Standard requests are processed within 2 business days.
  • SharePoint site storage increases require approval from your department head in addition to IT. Allow up to 3 business days.
  • You cannot directly increase your own quota — all increases must be requested through the Service Desk or the self-service portal.

Steps

Step 1 — Check Your Current OneDrive Storage Usage

  1. Go to https://robotix-my.sharepoint.com (sign in via Okta if prompted).
  2. In the lower-left corner of the page, your storage usage is displayed as a bar, e.g., "780 GB of 1 TB used."
  3. Alternatively, click the Settings gear > OneDrive settings > More settings > Storage metrics to see a breakdown by folder.

Step 2 — Check SharePoint Site Storage

  1. Go to the SharePoint site you manage or are a member of.
  2. Click Settings (gear) > Site information > View all site settings.
  3. Under Site Collection Administration, click Storage metrics to see current usage and quota.
  4. Only Site Owners and IT admins can view full storage metrics. If you are not a Site Owner, ask your Site Owner to check.

Step 3 — Free Up Space Before Requesting More

Before submitting a request, take these steps to reduce unnecessary storage use:

  • Delete large, outdated files: Sort your OneDrive by size (click the Size column header at https://robotix-my.sharepoint.com). Delete files you no longer need.
  • Empty the OneDrive Recycle Bin: Deleted files still count against your quota until the Recycle Bin is emptied. Go to https://robotix-my.sharepoint.com > Recycle bin > Empty recycle bin.
  • Move project archives to a team SharePoint site: If your personal OneDrive holds shared project files, move them to the appropriate Teams/SharePoint site so they are under the site's quota, not yours.
  • Review large files in Teams: Files shared in Teams channels are stored in SharePoint, not your personal OneDrive — these do not count against your personal 1 TB.

Step 4 — Submit a Storage Increase Request

Via the Self-Service Portal (Recommended): 1. Go to https://help.robotix.com and sign in with your Okta credentials. 2. Click Request a Service > Microsoft 365 > OneDrive / SharePoint Storage Increase. 3. Fill in the request form: - Request type: OneDrive personal storage OR SharePoint site storage - Current usage: Your current % used (from Step 1 or 2) - Amount requested: State how much additional storage you need (e.g., "500 GB additional" or "increase to 5 TB") - Business justification: Briefly explain why the additional storage is needed (e.g., "large video project files for the Q3 marketing campaign") - SharePoint site URL: Required for site storage requests 4. Click Submit. You will receive a ticket confirmation email with your REQ-###### number.

Via Email: Send a request to servicedesk@robotix.com with the subject line: OneDrive/SharePoint Storage Increase Request and include all details listed in Step 4 above.

Step 5 — What Happens After You Submit

  • You will receive a confirmation email with your ticket number.
  • For OneDrive personal storage: IT reviews and provisions the increase within 2 business days. You will receive an email when complete.
  • For SharePoint site storage: IT will contact your department head for approval, then provision the increase within 3 business days.
  • The storage increase will appear automatically in OneDrive or SharePoint — no action is required on your part once approved.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
Storage bar not updating after IT confirms increase Sign out of OneDrive and back in (see KB-0209 for sign-out steps); the new quota should refresh within 15 minutes.
Cannot find "Storage Metrics" in SharePoint You must be a Site Owner. Ask your Site Owner or IT to check it for you.
Recycle Bin won't empty Some files may be locked or in the second-stage recycle bin. Contact the Service Desk.
OneDrive shows "over quota" even after freeing up space OneDrive may take up to an hour to recalculate usage. Check back after an hour before submitting a request.

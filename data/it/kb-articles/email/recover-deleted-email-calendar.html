Recover a Deleted Email or Calendar Item

Summary

When you delete an email or calendar event in Outlook, it moves to the Deleted Items folder. If you empty that folder (or the item is auto-purged), it moves to the Recoverable Items folder, where it remains available for up to 30 days before permanent deletion. This article covers how to recover items from both locations.

Before You Begin

  • Items in the Deleted Items folder are easy to restore by moving them back to your Inbox.
  • Items purged from Deleted Items are held in Recoverable Items (Purges) for 30 calendar days per Robotix IT's retention policy.
  • After 30 days, items cannot be recovered by end users. Contact the Service Desk immediately if you need a recovery attempt after this window — compliance holds may allow longer recovery in exceptional cases.
  • Calendar items deleted within the last 30 days can be recovered using the same process.

Steps

Recover from Deleted Items (Standard Recovery)

Outlook on Windows or macOS: 1. In the left pane, click Deleted Items. 2. Find the message or calendar event you want to restore. Use the Search box within the folder to search by sender, subject, or date. 3. Right-click the item and choose Move > Inbox (for email) or Move > Calendar (for calendar events). 4. The item is immediately restored to its destination folder.

Outlook on the Web: 1. In the left sidebar, click Deleted Items. 2. Find the item and right-click it, then choose Move > Inbox or another destination folder.

Recover Permanently Deleted Items (Recoverable Items)

If an item is no longer in Deleted Items (because you emptied the folder or it was auto-purged):

Outlook on Windows: 1. Click on the Deleted Items folder in the left pane to select it. 2. On the Home tab in the ribbon, click Recover Deleted Items From Server (in the "Delete" group). If you don't see this, go to Folder tab > Recover Deleted Items. 3. A dialog box opens showing items in the Recoverable Items folder. Items here are within the 30-day retention window. 4. Select the item(s) you want to restore. Use Ctrl+click to select multiple. 5. Click Restore Selected Items (the arrow icon pointing to a folder). 6. Items are restored to your Deleted Items folder — from there, move them to your Inbox or Calendar.

Outlook on the Web: 1. In the left pane, scroll down to the bottom and click Deleted Items. 2. At the top of the Deleted Items folder view, click the link Recover items deleted from this folder (shown in a blue bar). 3. Select items to recover and click Restore.

Outlook on macOS: 1. On macOS, the "Recover Deleted Items from Server" option may not be available in all versions of Outlook. 2. Use Outlook on the Web (https://outlook.office.com) as the most reliable method for recovering purged items on macOS.

Recover a Deleted Calendar Event

Outlook on Windows: 1. Go to Calendar view. 2. Click on the Deleted Items folder in the left pane — deleted calendar events appear here alongside emails. 3. Right-click the event and choose Move > Calendar.

Outlook on the Web: 1. Go to https://outlook.office.com and click the Calendar icon. 2. Click Deleted Items in the left panel. 3. Select the deleted event and click Restore.

Recovering Items Older Than 30 Days

Items older than 30 days cannot be self-recovered. If you need a recovery attempt: 1. Submit a ticket at https://help.robotix.com. 2. Include: item type (email/calendar), approximate date, sender/subject, and the reason for recovery. 3. The Service Desk will escalate to the L3 team for a potential compliance mailbox recovery. Note: recovery is not guaranteed after the retention window.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
"Recover Deleted Items From Server" button is greyed out Make sure you have the Deleted Items folder selected (not Inbox) before clicking the button.
Restored email cannot be found in Inbox Check All Mail or search by subject — Outlook may have placed it in a subfolder.
Calendar event restored but not showing on calendar In Calendar view, check that the correct calendar is toggled on in the left pane.
Item is in Recoverable Items but restore fails Log out and back in to Outlook, then try again. If the issue persists, contact the Service Desk.

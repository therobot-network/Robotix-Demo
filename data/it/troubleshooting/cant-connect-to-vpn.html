I Can't Connect to the VPN

Use this guide to diagnose and resolve issues connecting to the Palo Alto GlobalProtect VPN, which is required for accessing Robotix IT internal resources from outside a Robotix IT office.

Start Here

Before working through this guide, confirm: - You are using a Robotix IT-managed laptop (Dell Latitude 7440 or MacBook Pro 14"). Personal devices cannot connect to GlobalProtect. - You have an active internet connection (test by loading https://google.com or another public site). - The GlobalProtect client is installed on your laptop (look for the globe icon in your system tray or menu bar). - Your VPN gateway address is vpn.robotix.com.

Identify the symptom: What exactly happens when you click Connect?

Step-by-Step Diagnostic Flow

1. Is the GlobalProtect client installed?

Windows: Look for the GlobalProtect globe icon in the system tray (bottom-right taskbar). If not visible, search Start for "GlobalProtect."

Mac: Look for the GlobalProtect icon in the menu bar (top-right). If not visible, check Applications for GlobalProtect.

If not installed → Open Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (Mac) and search for "GlobalProtect." Install and restart your laptop. If GlobalProtect is not available in the catalogue, submit a ticket at https://help.robotix.com for IT to push the client to your device.

If installed → Proceed to Step 2.

2. Is the gateway configured as vpn.robotix.com?

Open the GlobalProtect client and check the gateway field.

If blank or showing a different address → Click the gear icon (Settings) in the GlobalProtect client, enter vpn.robotix.com as the portal/gateway address, and click Connect.

If already set to vpn.robotix.com → Proceed to Step 3.

3. Do you have an active internet connection?

GlobalProtect needs internet access before it can establish the VPN tunnel.

Test: Open a browser and load https://google.com. If the page does not load, resolve your internet issue first — see TS-03: Wi-Fi or Internet Isn't Working. Your home router, ISP, or office Wi-Fi may need troubleshooting.

If internet is working → Proceed to Step 4.

4. What error does GlobalProtect display when you try to connect?

Match your error to the relevant step below:

  • "Invalid username or password" / "Authentication failed" → Proceed to Step 5.
  • "Unable to connect to the portal" / "Cannot reach the VPN gateway" → Proceed to Step 6.
  • "SSL Error" / "Certificate error" / "Untrusted site" → Proceed to Step 7.
  • Okta Verify push is not arriving → Proceed to Step 8.
  • Connection initiates but drops immediately or after a few seconds → Proceed to Step 9.
  • No error — client spins and eventually times out → Proceed to Step 6.

5. "Authentication failed" — are you using the correct Okta credentials?

Your GlobalProtect login uses the same credentials as your Okta account: firstname.lastname@robotix.com as the username, and your current Okta password.

  • Ensure Caps Lock is off.
  • Confirm your Okta password has not expired. Test by logging in at https://robotix.okta.com from a browser. If Okta prompts you to change your password, do so first — the new password must be used for GlobalProtect as well.
  • If your account is locked, unlock it via Okta self-service or contact the Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866 before retrying VPN.

Did correcting credentials resolve the issue?

If yes → Issue was an incorrect or expired password. Update your password in 1Password.

If no → Proceed to Step 8 (MFA issue) or contact the Service Desk.

6. "Cannot reach gateway" — is the VPN gateway reachable from your network?

Windows: Open Command Prompt and run: ping vpn.robotix.com

Mac: Open Terminal and run: ping -c 4 vpn.robotix.com

If ping fails (no response / "Request timed out") → - Your current network may be blocking VPN traffic (some hotels, coffee shops, and enterprise guest Wi-Fi block UDP 4501 and IPSEC). - Try switching to your mobile phone hotspot and reconnecting. If GlobalProtect connects on the hotspot, the original network is blocking VPN. - If on a corporate guest network at a customer site, VPN blocking is common — use your hotspot as the workaround. - If this is your home network, try restarting your router and modem.

If ping succeeds but VPN still fails → The issue is not network reachability — proceed to Step 9 or contact the Service Desk.

7. "SSL Error" or "Certificate error" when connecting.

Do not dismiss or click through certificate warnings. These may indicate: - You are on a network with a captive portal (hotel Wi-Fi) that intercepts SSL before you have accepted the portal terms. - A man-in-the-middle network condition.

For captive portals: Open a browser first and connect to the captive portal (accept the terms or log in to the hotel/venue Wi-Fi). Once your browser can load a public website, try GlobalProtect again.

If the certificate error persists on a trusted network: This may indicate a corrupted GlobalProtect certificate store. Submit a ticket at https://help.robotix.com — do not continue trying to bypass the error.

8. Okta Verify push is not arriving.

GlobalProtect uses Okta for authentication, which requires an Okta Verify push approval.

  1. Ensure your phone has an active internet connection (Wi-Fi or cellular data). Okta Verify cannot receive pushes without internet.
  2. Open the Okta Verify app manually. If a push is waiting, it will appear in the app even if the notification did not arrive.
  3. In the GlobalProtect authentication screen, look for a "Use code instead" or "Enter code" option. Open Okta Verify, tap your Robotix IT account, and use the displayed 6-digit time-based code (TOTP) as an alternative to the push.
  4. Ensure notifications are enabled for Okta Verify in your phone's settings (Settings > Notifications > Okta Verify — enable and allow sounds/banners).

Did using the TOTP code allow the VPN to connect?

If yes → Push notifications may be delayed or blocked on your phone. Check notification settings and report the issue to the Service Desk for MFA troubleshooting.

If no, code also fails → Your Okta Verify enrollment may be corrupted. Contact the Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866 for an MFA reset.

9. VPN connects but drops immediately or after a short period.

Restart GlobalProtect: 1. Windows: Right-click the GlobalProtect icon in the system tray, select Disconnect, wait 30 seconds, then select Connect again. 2. Mac: Click the GlobalProtect menu bar icon, select Disconnect, wait 30 seconds, then Connect.

Check for conflicting software: - Other VPN clients (e.g., Cisco AnyConnect, OpenVPN) running simultaneously can conflict with GlobalProtect. Disconnect and close any other VPN clients before connecting to GlobalProtect.

Test on mobile hotspot: - If drops are frequent on your home network but not on a hotspot, your home router may have an issue with long-lived VPN connections (some routers have aggressive NAT timeout settings). Try updating your router firmware or adjusting the NAT timeout settings (contact your router manufacturer for guidance).

Did any of the above resolve the dropping connection?

If yes → Note which fix resolved it for future reference.

If no → Collect the GlobalProtect connection log (Windows: %AppData%\Palo Alto Networks\GlobalProtect\PanGPA.log; Mac: /Library/Logs/PaloAltoNetworks/GlobalProtect/PanGPA.log) and submit a ticket at https://help.robotix.com, attaching the log file.

10. Reinstall the GlobalProtect client.

If all above steps fail, a corrupt installation may be the cause.

Windows: Go to Settings > Apps, uninstall GlobalProtect, restart your laptop, open Company Portal, and reinstall GlobalProtect.

Mac: Drag GlobalProtect from Applications to Trash. Also remove /Library/Application Support/Palo Alto Networks/ if present (requires admin access — contact Service Desk if needed). Restart, then reinstall via Self Service.

  • [ ] Tried authenticating with 6-digit TOTP code from Okta Verify (not just push)
  • [ ] Checked that phone has internet access for Okta Verify
  • [ ] Tested connection using mobile phone hotspot to rule out network blocking

  • [ ] Tried disconnecting/reconnecting GlobalProtect
  • [ ] Checked for a captive portal if connecting from a hotel or venue

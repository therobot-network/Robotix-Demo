Security & Phishing FAQ

What to do when something looks suspicious, how to report threats, and how Robotix IT protects your device and data.

How do I report a suspicious email (phishing)?

Use the Report Phishing button built into Microsoft Outlook on desktop and web — this sends the email directly to the Robotix IT Security Operations Center (SOC) for analysis. Alternatively, forward the suspicious email as an attachment to soc@robotix.com. Do not click any links, open attachments, or reply to the sender before reporting.

What are the signs that an email might be phishing?

Common indicators include: a sender address that doesn't match the claimed organization (e.g., ceo@robotix-corp.net instead of @robotix.com), urgent language demanding immediate action, requests for login credentials or financial information, mismatched or suspicious links (hover over links to preview the URL before clicking), and unexpected attachments — especially .zip, .exe, or Office files with macros.

I clicked a link in a suspicious email — what should I do immediately?

Do not enter any credentials on the page you were taken to. Disconnect your laptop from the internet immediately (turn off Wi-Fi and unplug any ethernet cable). Call the Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866 and explain what happened — this will be treated as a P1 or P2 security incident. Change your Okta password from a different device at https://robotix.okta.com. Do not restart or shut down your laptop until IT instructs you to.

What is CrowdStrike Falcon and what does it do on my laptop?

CrowdStrike Falcon is Robotix IT's endpoint security platform, pre-installed on all managed Windows and Mac laptops. It provides real-time threat detection, malware prevention, and behavioral monitoring. It runs silently in the background and does not require any action from you. Do not attempt to disable or uninstall it — doing so violates Robotix IT security policy and will trigger an alert.

Will I be notified if CrowdStrike detects something on my device?

Yes. If CrowdStrike detects and blocks a threat, you will see a notification on your screen. The SOC is also alerted simultaneously. Do not ignore these notifications — contact the Service Desk immediately at +1 (800) 555-7866 or via soc@robotix.com even if the notification says the threat was "blocked."

What should I do if I think my computer has a virus or malware?

Stop what you are doing, disconnect from the internet (disable Wi-Fi, unplug ethernet), and call the Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866. Do not try to run antivirus scans yourself or delete suspected files — the security team needs to collect forensic evidence before remediation. CrowdStrike Falcon is monitoring continuously, but suspicious behavior should always be reported.

I received an unexpected Okta Verify push notification asking me to approve a login — what should I do?

Deny it immediately and do not approve it. An unsolicited push notification means someone has your Okta password and is attempting to log in. Change your Okta password immediately at https://robotix.okta.com from a trusted device, then notify the SOC at soc@robotix.com. This is a credential compromise scenario and must be reported.

What is multi-factor authentication (MFA) and why is it required?

MFA requires a second form of verification — beyond your password — to prove your identity. At Robotix IT, this is done via Okta Verify push notifications on your enrolled phone. MFA is required for all Okta logins because passwords alone can be stolen through phishing or data breaches. Even if an attacker has your password, they cannot access your account without also controlling your phone.

Are there any exceptions where Robotix IT IT staff will ask for my password?

No. Legitimate Robotix IT IT staff, including the Service Desk, will never ask for your Okta password, 1Password master password, or any other credential via email, phone, chat, or in person. If anyone asks for your password claiming to be IT, refuse and report it to soc@robotix.com immediately.

What is a "vishing" or phone-based phishing attack, and how do I recognize it?

Vishing is voice phishing — a caller pretends to be IT support, a vendor, or an authority figure and tries to get you to reveal credentials, install software, or grant access. Red flags: the caller creates urgency ("your account will be locked in 10 minutes"), claims to need your password to fix an issue, or asks you to install remote control software. Hang up and call the Service Desk directly at +1 (800) 555-7866 to verify.

Can I install browser extensions or plugins on my work laptop?

Only browser extensions vetted and approved by IT are permitted. Do not install extensions from unknown sources — malicious extensions can steal credentials and session tokens. If you need a specific extension for work, submit a request at https://help.robotix.com. The IT team will review and approve or deny within the standard SLA.

What happens if I fail a Robotix IT security awareness phishing simulation?

Robotix IT periodically sends simulated phishing emails to test awareness. If you click a simulated phishing link, you will be redirected to a brief security awareness training module that must be completed. There is no penalty — these simulations exist to help you recognize attacks before a real one occurs. Completion of the training is tracked.

How do I securely share sensitive files or data with an external party?

Use OneDrive to create a secure sharing link with an expiration date and password protection, or use the approved secure file transfer tool listed in the RobotixDesk knowledge base. Do not email sensitive files as unencrypted attachments or use personal file-sharing services (Dropbox, Google Drive, WeTransfer). Contact soc@robotix.com if you are unsure whether your intended sharing method is approved.

What is the Robotix IT acceptable use policy for work devices?

Work devices (laptops and mobile devices managed by Intune/Jamf) are for business use. Incidental personal use is permitted, but you must not: install unauthorized software, disable security controls, connect to untrusted networks without VPN, share your credentials, or use company devices for illegal activities. The full Acceptable Use Policy is available in the RobotixDesk knowledge base at https://help.robotix.com.

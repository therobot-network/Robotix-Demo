Headset or Audio Isn't Working

Summary

This article helps resolve issues with Jabra and Logitech headsets issued by Summit IT, including no sound, microphone not detected, and poor call audio quality in Microsoft Teams and Zoom. It also covers the laptop's built-in speakers and microphone as fallback devices.

Before You Begin

  • Identify your headset model (printed on the headset or on the box): common Summit-issued models include the Jabra Evolve2 55, Jabra Engage 55, Logitech Zone Wireless, and Logitech H390 (USB wired).
  • Confirm how the headset is connected: USB-A cable, USB-C, Bluetooth, or wireless USB receiver (Jabra Link 380 or Logitech Bolt dongle).
  • Muted hardware buttons on the headset itself are the most common cause of "no audio" calls — check the mute button on the headset arm or earcup before proceeding.

Steps

Step 1 — Check Physical Connections and Headset Controls

  1. Check headset mute. Most Jabra and Logitech headsets have a dedicated mute button (often with a red LED indicator when muted). Press it once to unmute.
  2. Check headset volume. Press the volume-up button on the headset or scroll wheel and confirm you can hear a tone or click.
  3. For wired USB headsets (e.g., Logitech H390): Unplug the USB connector, wait 5 seconds, and replug. Try a different USB port on the dock or directly on the laptop.
  4. For wireless headsets with a USB dongle (e.g., Jabra Evolve2 55, Logitech Zone Wireless): Confirm the dongle is plugged into the dock or laptop. Power cycle the headset by turning it off and back on. Check the headset's charge level — some models will not connect if battery is below 10%.

Step 2 — Set the Headset as the Default Audio Device

Windows 11:

  1. Click the speaker icon in the taskbar system tray. Click the arrow next to the volume slider to open the sound output selector.
  2. Select your headset (e.g., Jabra Evolve2 55) from the list.
  3. Click the microphone icon in the taskbar (or go to Settings > System > Sound) and under Input, select your headset microphone.
  4. Under Settings > System > Sound, scroll down to Advanced > More sound settings. On the Playback tab, right-click your headset and select Set as Default Device and also Set as Default Communication Device.
  5. Do the same on the Recording tab for the headset microphone.

macOS:

  1. Go to Apple menu > System Settings > Sound.
  2. Under Output, select your headset.
  3. Under Input, select your headset microphone.
  4. Adjust the Input Volume slider to approximately 75% if the microphone level bar is not moving when you speak.

Step 3 — Set the Headset in Teams and Zoom

Microsoft Teams:

  1. In Teams, click your profile picture > Settings > Devices.
  2. Under Audio devices, select your headset from the Speaker and Microphone drop-downs (e.g., "Jabra Evolve2 55" or "Logitech Zone Wireless").
  3. Click Make a test call to verify audio in both directions.

Zoom:

  1. In Zoom, click your profile picture > Settings > Audio.
  2. Under Speaker, select your headset from the drop-down. Click Test Speaker to play a tone.
  3. Under Microphone, select your headset microphone. Click Test Mic and speak — you should see the input level bar move.
  4. Ensure Automatically adjust microphone volume is checked if your mic sounds too quiet.

Step 4 — Update Headset Firmware / Software

  1. Jabra headsets: Install Jabra Direct from the Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (macOS). Open Jabra Direct, connect your headset, and click Update if a firmware update is available.
  2. Logitech headsets: Install Logi Tune from the Company Portal or Self Service. Open Logi Tune, select your headset, and apply any available updates.
  3. After updating, restart the headset and retest.

Step 5 — Test with Built-in Speakers and Microphone

  1. As a fallback, switch audio to the laptop's built-in speaker and microphone using the steps in Step 2, selecting the built-in devices instead of the headset.
  2. If the built-in devices work but the headset does not, the headset may be faulty — submit a repair request (KB-0407).

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Action
Others can't hear you Headset mic muted or wrong input device selected Unmute headset hardware mute button; confirm headset mic is set as input in OS and app settings
You can't hear others Wrong output device or headset not connected Select headset as output device in OS sound settings and Teams/Zoom settings
Audio works in Teams but not Zoom (or vice versa) App-level audio device setting not updated Set headset in both Teams Settings > Devices and Zoom Settings > Audio
Headset microphone sounds echo-y for others Mic picking up speaker output Enable noise suppression in Teams (Settings > Devices > Noise suppression: High); in Zoom, enable background noise suppression
Jabra headset not appearing in Jabra Direct Dongle not connected or firmware mismatch Replug the Jabra Link dongle; reinstall Jabra Direct
Headset keeps disconnecting every few minutes USB power management (Windows) See KB-0404 Troubleshooting — disable USB selective suspend for the headset port
No audio after docking/undocking Windows auto-switched to wrong output device Click speaker icon in taskbar and manually reselect headset

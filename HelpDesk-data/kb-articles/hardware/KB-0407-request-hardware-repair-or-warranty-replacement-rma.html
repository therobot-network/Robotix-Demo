Request a Hardware Repair or Warranty Replacement (RMA)

Summary

This article explains how to submit a hardware repair request for a Summit IT-issued device that has a confirmed defect or physical damage. IT handles RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) coordination with Dell for Latitude laptops and Apple for MacBook Pros, as well as repairs for monitors, docks, keyboards, mice, and headsets. You do not need to contact Dell or Apple directly — Summit IT manages all vendor escalations on your behalf.

Before You Begin

  • Perform basic troubleshooting first. RMA requests submitted before basic troubleshooting may be redirected back for further diagnosis. Refer to the relevant article for your issue:
  • Power issues: KB-0401 (won't power on), KB-0402 (battery)
  • Display issues: KB-0403 (monitors/dock)
  • Peripherals: KB-0404 (keyboard/mouse), KB-0405 (webcam), KB-0406 (headset)
  • Have your asset tag ready. This is the white barcode label on the underside of the device: SUM-LT-##### (Dell Latitude), SUM-MB-##### (MacBook Pro), SUM-MN-##### (monitor).
  • If the damage was caused by accidental damage (e.g., drop, liquid spill), IT will note this in the ticket. Accidental damage may be subject to manager approval for repair costs.
  • Back up your data before submitting the device for repair. IT will attempt to preserve data, but cannot guarantee data retention if the device requires a logic board or SSD replacement. Use OneDrive (Windows) or OneDrive/SharePoint (macOS) to sync important files before handoff.

Steps

  1. Open the Summit Service Desk portal at https://help.summitit.com and sign in with your Okta credentials.
  2. Click Request a Service and select Hardware & Equipment > Hardware Repair / RMA Request.
  3. Complete all required fields: - Asset tag: Enter the full SUM-LT-##### / SUM-MB-##### / SUM-MN-##### value. - Device type: Laptop, Monitor, Dock, Keyboard, Mouse, or Headset. - Issue description: Provide a specific description of the fault. Include what steps you have already tried, when the problem started, and any error codes or LED patterns observed. Vague descriptions (e.g., "it doesn't work") slow down diagnosis. - Damage type: Defect (no physical damage) or Accidental Damage (drop, spill, crack, etc.). - Impact on work: Describe how the issue affects your productivity. This determines ticket priority (P2–P4).
  4. Attach photos of any visible physical damage to the ticket. Photos can be taken on your phone and uploaded directly.
  5. Click Submit. You will receive a confirmation email with a ticket ID (format: INC-######).
  6. An IT technician will contact you within the SLA window to arrange one of the following: - In-office repair: IT will schedule a time to collect and assess the device at your office location. - Remote diagnosis: A technician will join remotely to run diagnostics before determining if a physical repair is needed. - Advance replacement: For confirmed hardware defects under Dell or Apple warranty, IT may ship a replacement device before receiving the faulty one (subject to availability).
  7. When instructed to hand over the device: - Remove any personal USB drives, privacy screens, or accessories from the device. - Note any pre-existing cosmetic damage not related to the fault — inform the technician so it is documented. - Receive a loaner laptop if your repair will take more than 1 business day (see KB-0408 for the loaner process).
  8. IT will notify you when the repaired or replacement device is ready. You will receive a new asset tag sticker if a replacement device is issued.

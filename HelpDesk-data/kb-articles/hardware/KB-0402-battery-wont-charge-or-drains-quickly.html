Battery Won't Charge or Drains Quickly

Summary

This article covers two related battery problems: a laptop battery that does not charge at all, and a battery that charges but depletes unusually fast. Both issues can stem from software, firmware, power settings, or a worn battery cell. Follow the platform-specific steps below before requesting a hardware repair.

Before You Begin

  • Have your laptop's asset tag ready (SUM-LT-##### for Dell Latitude 7440, SUM-MB-##### for MacBook Pro 14").
  • Note approximately how old the device is — batteries are typically warranted for 1 year and rated for around 500 full charge cycles before noticeable degradation.
  • Close non-essential applications before running diagnostics; background processes can inflate apparent power drain.

Steps

Dell Latitude 7440 (Windows 11)

If the battery is not charging:

  1. Disconnect the USB-C power adapter from the Dell WD19 dock and connect it directly to the laptop's USB-C port. The dock requires its own power brick; if the dock's power brick is missing or unplugged from the wall, the laptop will not charge through it.
  2. Confirm the power brick's LED is solid white. Amber or off indicates a fault with the adapter or wall outlet. Try a different outlet.
  3. In Windows, click the battery icon in the taskbar. If it shows "Plugged in, not charging", this can be a temporary firmware protection state. Shut down the laptop fully (Start > Shut down), leave the power adapter connected, wait 60 seconds, then power on.
  4. Open Device Manager (right-click Start > Device Manager), expand Batteries, and confirm both Microsoft AC Adapter and Microsoft ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery are listed without warning icons. If a warning icon appears, right-click the item and select Uninstall device. Reboot — Windows will reinstall the driver automatically.
  5. Check the Dell Power Manager app (search for it in Start). Under Battery, review the battery health status. If health is reported below 50%, the battery should be replaced — submit a repair request (KB-0407).

If the battery drains too quickly:

  1. Open Settings > System > Power & battery > Battery usage and review which apps consumed the most power in the last 24 hours. Close or uninstall high-drain applications that are not needed.
  2. Go to Settings > System > Power & battery and set the power mode to Balanced rather than Best performance when unplugged.
  3. Ensure the display brightness is not set to 100% — reduce to 60–70% for typical indoor use.
  4. Disconnect unnecessary peripherals (external drives, USB hubs) when running on battery.
  5. In Dell Power Manager, enable Battery Extender mode, which limits background activity when on battery.

MacBook Pro 14" (Apple silicon)

If the battery is not charging:

  1. Disconnect the USB-C cable and inspect both ends and the port on the MacBook for debris or damage. Use a flashlight. Do not insert metal objects to clear debris — contact IT if the port appears obstructed.
  2. Reconnect the cable. If using MagSafe, the LED should glow amber within 30 seconds. If no LED, try a different Apple USB-C cable.
  3. Try a different wall outlet and, if available, a different USB-C power adapter.
  4. On the Mac, go to Apple menu > System Settings > Battery. Check the Battery Health field. If it shows Service Recommended, the battery needs replacement — submit a repair request (KB-0407).
  5. Reset the battery charging state: go to Apple menu > Shut Down, wait 30 seconds with the power adapter connected, then power on.

If the battery drains too quickly:

  1. Go to Apple menu > System Settings > Battery and review the Battery Usage section. Look for apps listed as using Significant Energy.
  2. Enable Low Power Mode when unplugged for extended battery life.
  3. Check System Settings > Displays and lower display brightness.
  4. In System Settings > Battery, ensure Optimized Battery Charging is turned on. This feature learns your charge pattern and reduces wear.
  5. Run Activity Monitor (Applications > Utilities > Activity Monitor), click the Energy tab, and close any apps with unusually high Energy Impact values.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Action
"Plugged in, not charging" on Windows with a full battery Battery protection threshold reached Battery is set to stop at 80% by design; this is normal if Dell Power Manager's "primarily AC" mode is enabled
Battery percentage jumps erratically Worn battery cell Check health in Dell Power Manager or macOS Battery settings; request replacement if health is below 50%
Mac shows "Service Recommended" in Battery Health Battery at end of life Submit RMA/repair request via KB-0407
Laptop gets hot while charging and drains fast Thermal throttling or background update Ensure vents are unobstructed; check for large Windows Update or macOS update running in background
Charge drops from 100% to 20% in under 2 hours High-drain workload or battery degradation Review app power usage; if usage is normal, request battery replacement

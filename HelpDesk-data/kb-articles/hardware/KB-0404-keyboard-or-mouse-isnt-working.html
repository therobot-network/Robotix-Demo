Keyboard or Mouse Isn't Working

Summary

This article covers troubleshooting for Logitech keyboards and mice (wired, wireless USB receiver, or Bluetooth) that are unresponsive or behaving erratically. It applies to all Logitech peripherals issued by Summit IT, including the MX Keys keyboard and MX Master mouse series.

Before You Begin

  • Identify whether your keyboard/mouse is wired, wireless with a USB receiver (Unifying or Logi Bolt), or Bluetooth.
  • Check the battery level. Logitech wireless peripherals use AA or AAA batteries (some rechargeable models use USB-C). Low battery is the most common cause of an unresponsive device.
  • If the device is connected through the Dell WD19 dock, test it plugged directly into the laptop first to isolate whether the dock or the device itself is the problem.

Steps

Wireless Keyboard or Mouse (USB Receiver)

  1. Check the power switch. Logitech MX Master and MX Keys devices have a power switch on the underside. Confirm it is in the On position.
  2. Replace or recharge the battery. Open the battery compartment or connect the USB-C charging cable. Allow 5 minutes of charge before retesting.
  3. Remove and reinsert the USB receiver. Unplug the Unifying or Logi Bolt USB receiver from the dock or laptop, wait 10 seconds, then plug it back in. Try a different USB port on the laptop if available.
  4. Move the receiver closer to the device. If the receiver is on the back of the dock, USB interference from other devices can reduce range. Use the Logi Bolt USB extender cable (small orange cable included in the box) to position the receiver on the desktop within 30 cm of the device.
  5. Re-pair the receiver (Logi Bolt). If your device uses the newer Logi Bolt receiver (identified by the orange lightning-bolt logo): - Install Logi Bolt app from the Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (macOS). - Open Logi Bolt and click Add Device. Follow the on-screen instructions to re-pair.
  6. Re-pair the receiver (Unifying). If your device uses the older Unifying receiver (orange star logo): - Install Logitech Unifying Software from the Company Portal or Self Service. - Open the app, click Advanced, then follow the pairing wizard.

Bluetooth Keyboard or Mouse

  1. Toggle Bluetooth off and on. On Windows: Settings > Bluetooth & devices, toggle Bluetooth off, wait 5 seconds, toggle back on. On macOS: System Settings > Bluetooth, toggle off and on.
  2. Remove and re-add the device. - Windows: Go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices, find the device, click the ... menu, select Remove device, then click Add device and re-pair. - macOS: Go to System Settings > Bluetooth, click the X next to the device to forget it, then hold the pairing button on the device and select it from the list.
  3. Switch the device's Bluetooth channel. Logitech MX devices support up to 3 paired devices on channels 1, 2, and 3 (button on the underside of the device). Press the button to cycle channels and confirm you are on the correct channel for your laptop.

Wired Keyboard or Mouse

  1. Try a different USB port. If connected to the dock, try the laptop's built-in USB-A or USB-C ports directly.
  2. Try a different cable. If the keyboard uses a detachable cable, test with a different USB-A to USB-C cable.
  3. Check for driver issues (Windows). Open Device Manager (right-click Start > Device Manager), expand Human Interface Devices and Keyboards/Mice. If any device shows a yellow warning icon, right-click it and select Update driver > Search automatically.

Built-in Laptop Keyboard (Dell Latitude 7440)

  1. Restart the laptop. A stalled keyboard driver is common after sleep/wake cycles — a restart usually resolves it.
  2. Disable and re-enable the keyboard driver. In Device Manager > Keyboards, right-click Standard PS/2 Keyboard, select Disable device, wait 10 seconds, then Enable device.
  3. Check for stuck keys. A key physically stuck down can block other inputs. Press each key to confirm it springs back. If a key is physically damaged, submit a repair request (KB-0407).

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Action
Cursor moves but clicks do nothing Mouse button may be in drag-lock mode Press Escape and click once. Check Logitech Options+ app for any assigned gestures.
Keyboard types wrong characters Incorrect keyboard layout Windows: Settings > Time & Language > Typing > Advanced keyboard settings. macOS: System Settings > Keyboard > Input Sources.
Mouse scrolling is reversed Scroll direction setting Windows: Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Mouse > Scroll direction. macOS: System Settings > Mouse > Natural scrolling.
Device connects but disconnects every few minutes USB power management suspending the port Windows: Device Manager > Universal Serial Bus controllers > right-click each USB Root Hub > Properties > Power Management > uncheck "Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power."
Logi Bolt software not installing Software not approved yet Open a ticket with IT to request Logi Bolt app approval; in the interim, re-pair via Unifying Software.

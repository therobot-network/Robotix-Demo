Install and Connect GlobalProtect VPN on Windows

Summary

Summit IT uses Palo Alto GlobalProtect as its VPN solution. You must be connected to GlobalProtect when working off the corporate network to access internal websites, shared drives, and business applications. This article covers downloading, installing, and making your first connection on Windows 11.

Before You Begin

  • You need an active Summit IT account (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) with Okta credentials.
  • Okta Verify must be installed and enrolled on your phone — GlobalProtect triggers an MFA push during authentication.
  • If your device was issued by Summit IT and is enrolled in Microsoft Intune, GlobalProtect may already be installed via Company Portal. Check by searching for "GlobalProtect" in the Start menu before proceeding with a manual install.
  • You do not need GlobalProtect when you are already on the Summit-Corp Wi-Fi network at the office.

Steps

Part 1 — Install GlobalProtect

Option A: Install via Company Portal (recommended for corporate devices)

  1. Click Start and open Company Portal.
  2. Search for GlobalProtect in the search bar.
  3. Click GlobalProtect in the results and select Install.
  4. Wait for the installation to complete (typically 2–3 minutes).
  5. Proceed to Part 2.

Option B: Download directly from the IT portal

  1. From a machine with internet access, open a browser and go to https://help.summitit.com.
  2. Sign in with your Okta credentials.
  3. Navigate to Software Downloads > VPN > GlobalProtect — Windows 64-bit.
  4. Download the .msi installer file.
  5. Double-click the downloaded file to launch the installer.
  6. Click Next on the welcome screen.
  7. Accept the license agreement and click Next.
  8. Leave the installation path at the default (C:\Program Files\Palo Alto Networks\GlobalProtect) and click Next.
  9. Click Install. If prompted by User Account Control, click Yes.
  10. Click Finish when the installer completes.

Part 2 — Connect for the First Time

  1. Click the GlobalProtect icon in the system tray (it looks like a globe). If you don't see it, search for GlobalProtect in the Start menu and open the app.
  2. In the GlobalProtect panel, the Portal field should already be pre-filled with vpn.summitit.com. If it is blank, type vpn.summitit.com and press Enter.
  3. Click Connect.
  4. A browser window opens for Okta authentication. Enter your Summit IT email address and Okta password, then click Sign In.
  5. Approve the Okta Verify push notification on your phone.
  6. The browser will close and GlobalProtect will finish connecting. The globe icon in the system tray turns green and displays Connected.

Part 3 — Disconnect

  1. Click the GlobalProtect icon in the system tray.
  2. Click Disconnect.
  3. The icon returns to grey when disconnected.

Note: GlobalProtect is configured to reconnect automatically when you open your laptop away from the office. You generally do not need to manually initiate a connection each time.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
Portal field is blank after install Pre-config not yet applied Type vpn.summitit.com manually and press Enter
Okta login page doesn't open Browser pop-up blocked Allow pop-ups from vpn.summitit.com in your browser settings
"Authentication failed" Wrong Okta password Reset at https://help.summitit.com or contact the Service Desk
MFA push not received Phone offline or Okta Verify issue Check phone connectivity; open Okta Verify and tap Refresh; use the OTP code instead
Stuck on "Connecting…" Network or firewall issue See KB-0304 — GlobalProtect VPN Won't Connect
Globe icon is grey after connecting VPN tunnel dropped Click the icon and select Reconnect
GlobalProtect not found in Company Portal Device not enrolled in Intune Contact the Service Desk to enroll your device (REQ ticket)

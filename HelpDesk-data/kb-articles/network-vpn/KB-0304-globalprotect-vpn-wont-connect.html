GlobalProtect VPN Won't Connect — Troubleshooting

Summary

This article covers the most common reasons Palo Alto GlobalProtect fails to connect and provides step-by-step fixes for each scenario. Work through the sections below in order. If you are unable to resolve the issue after following these steps, contact the Summit Service Desk with the error message and the GlobalProtect diagnostic log.

Before You Begin

  • Note the exact error message GlobalProtect displays — this is the fastest way to identify the cause.
  • Confirm whether the issue is happening on a specific network (e.g., only at home, only on hotel Wi-Fi) or on all networks.
  • Have your Okta username (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) and password ready.
  • Know the portal address: vpn.summitit.com.

Steps

Step 1 — Verify you are not already on the corporate network

GlobalProtect will not connect if your device is already on the Summit-Corp office Wi-Fi or wired network, because VPN is not needed on-site. This is by design.

  • If you are at the office, disconnect GlobalProtect — you are already protected.
  • If you are remote, proceed to the next step.

Step 2 — Check basic internet connectivity

GlobalProtect requires a working internet connection before it can reach the VPN gateway.

  1. Open a browser and try loading https://www.google.com.
  2. If the page does not load, troubleshoot your local internet first (restart your router/modem, or contact your ISP). See also KB-0309 (home network tips).
  3. Once you have a working internet connection, retry GlobalProtect.

Step 3 — Confirm the portal address is correct

  1. Open GlobalProtect (click the globe icon in the system tray or menu bar).
  2. Verify the Portal field shows exactly: vpn.summitit.com
  3. If it shows anything else, clear the field, type vpn.summitit.com, and click Connect.

Step 4 — Re-enter your Okta credentials

  1. When the Okta browser sign-in page appears, enter your full Summit IT email (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) and current Okta password.
  2. If you see "Invalid username or password," go to https://help.summitit.com > Reset My Password before retrying.

Step 5 — Resolve MFA / Okta Verify issues

GlobalProtect requires MFA approval. If the Okta Verify push does not arrive:

  1. Open the Okta Verify app on your phone.
  2. Pull down to refresh the account.
  3. Instead of waiting for a push, tap the account to reveal the 6-digit TOTP code and enter it in the Okta sign-in browser window.
  4. If Okta Verify shows an error or your account is missing, contact the Service Desk — your MFA enrollment may need to be reset.

Step 6 — Restart the GlobalProtect service

Windows 11: 1. Press Win + R, type services.msc, and press Enter. 2. Scroll to PanGPS (Palo Alto GlobalProtect Service). 3. Right-click > Restart. 4. Retry connecting GlobalProtect.

macOS: 1. Open Terminal (Spotlight: Cmd + Space, type "Terminal"). 2. Run: sudo launchctl stop com.paloaltonetworks.gpe.pangpsd sudo launchctl start com.paloaltonetworks.gpe.pangpsd 3. Enter your Mac password if prompted. 4. Retry GlobalProtect.

Step 7 — Check for firewall or proxy interference

Some home routers, hotel networks, or third-party security software block VPN traffic.

  • Home router: Ensure UDP port 4501 (IPSec/NAT-T) and TCP port 443 are not blocked. Check your router's firewall settings or temporarily disable it for testing.
  • Third-party software: Temporarily disable any personal VPN, antivirus firewall, or network filtering software, then retry GlobalProtect.
  • Hotel/café networks: Some restrict VPN traffic. Ask the front desk if outbound VPN is allowed, or use your phone as a mobile hotspot.

Step 8 — Reinstall GlobalProtect

If none of the above steps work, reinstall the application:

  • Windows: Go to Settings > Apps > search for GlobalProtect > Uninstall. Then reinstall from Company Portal (KB-0302, Option A).
  • macOS: Drag GlobalProtect from Applications to Trash, then reinstall via Jamf Self Service (KB-0303, Option A).

Step 9 — Collect diagnostic logs for the Service Desk

Windows: 1. Right-click the GlobalProtect icon in the system tray. 2. Select Get Support or Collect Logs. 3. A ZIP file is saved to your Desktop. Attach it to your Service Desk ticket.

macOS: 1. Click the GlobalProtect icon in the menu bar. 2. Click the settings gear > Get Support. 3. A .tar.gz file is saved to your Downloads folder. Attach it to your ticket.

Common Error Messages

Error Message Meaning Fix
"Could not connect to the server" Portal unreachable Check internet; confirm portal is vpn.summitit.com
"Authentication failed" Wrong Okta password Reset password at https://help.summitit.com
"Pre-logon authentication failed" Machine certificate issue Contact Service Desk — L2 required
"Gateway not responding" VPN gateway overloaded or down Wait 5 minutes and retry; check #it-help for outage notices
"Connection timed out" Firewall/port blocking Try mobile hotspot; see Step 7 above
"Unable to establish IPsec tunnel" UDP 4501 blocked See Step 7 — router or ISP blocking VPN traffic
"Network error, please retry" Intermittent connectivity Retry; if persistent, restart the GlobalProtect service (Step 6)

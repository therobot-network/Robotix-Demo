Install and Connect GlobalProtect VPN on Mac

Summary

Summit IT uses Palo Alto GlobalProtect for remote access. When you are working outside the office — at home, a café, a client site, or any network other than Summit-Corp — GlobalProtect creates an encrypted tunnel so your Mac can reach internal applications, file shares, and other corporate resources. This article covers installing and connecting GlobalProtect on macOS Sonoma (14) and Sequoia (15).

Before You Begin

  • You need an active Summit IT account (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) with your current Okta password.
  • Okta Verify must be installed and enrolled on your iPhone or another device — you will need to approve an MFA push to complete sign-in.
  • If your Mac was issued by Summit IT, it is enrolled in Jamf Pro. GlobalProtect is likely already installed. Open Self Service (search with Spotlight: Cmd + Space, type "Self Service") and check before downloading manually.
  • GlobalProtect requires Full Disk Access and a System Extension on macOS. The installer will prompt you to grant these. Have your Mac's local administrator credentials ready if your account is not a local admin — contact the Service Desk if you are unsure.

Steps

Part 1 — Install GlobalProtect

Option A: Install via Jamf Self Service (recommended for Summit IT-issued Macs)

  1. Open Finder > Applications > Self Service, or search for it with Spotlight.
  2. In Self Service, click the Browse tab and search for GlobalProtect.
  3. Click Install next to GlobalProtect.
  4. Self Service installs the app silently. It will appear in your Applications folder when done.
  5. Proceed to Part 2.

Option B: Download from the IT portal

  1. Open Safari and go to https://help.summitit.com.
  2. Sign in with your Okta credentials.
  3. Go to Software Downloads > VPN > GlobalProtect — macOS.
  4. Download the .pkg installer.
  5. Double-click the .pkg file in your Downloads folder.
  6. Click Continue on the Introduction screen.
  7. Click Continue on the License screen, then Agree.
  8. Click Install. Enter your Mac's local administrator password if prompted.
  9. When macOS shows a System Extension Blocked notification, click Open System Settings. - In System Settings > Privacy & Security, scroll down and click Allow next to the Palo Alto Networks message.
  10. Click Close in the installer when it finishes.

Grant Full Disk Access (if prompted)

  1. Open System Settings > Privacy & Security > Full Disk Access.
  2. Click the + button and add GlobalProtect from Applications.
  3. Toggle it on.

Part 2 — Connect for the First Time

  1. Click the GlobalProtect icon in the menu bar (top-right — it looks like a globe). If you don't see it, open Finder > Applications > GlobalProtect.
  2. The GlobalProtect panel opens. The Portal Address field should read vpn.summitit.com. If it is blank, type vpn.summitit.com.
  3. Click Connect.
  4. Your default browser opens an Okta sign-in page. Enter your Summit IT email and Okta password, then click Sign In.
  5. Approve the Okta Verify push notification on your phone.
  6. The browser window closes and GlobalProtect displays Connected. The globe icon in the menu bar turns green.

Part 3 — Disconnect

  1. Click the GlobalProtect icon in the menu bar.
  2. Click Disconnect.
  3. The globe icon returns to its outline state (not filled/green).

Tip: GlobalProtect is set to reconnect automatically when your Mac wakes from sleep away from the office. You should not need to manually connect each time you open the lid.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
GlobalProtect not in Applications or Self Service Mac not enrolled in Jamf Contact the Service Desk — your Mac may need enrollment
System extension blocked, can't allow it MDM restriction in place This is usually auto-approved on Jamf-managed Macs; wait 5 minutes and retry, or contact the Service Desk
Portal address field is blank Pre-config not yet applied Manually type vpn.summitit.com and press Return
Browser sign-in page opens but spins Safari content blocker or network issue Try in a different browser; disable any content blockers on vpn.summitit.com
"Authentication failed" Incorrect Okta password Go to https://help.summitit.com and reset your password
MFA push not arriving Phone not connected or Okta Verify needs refresh Open Okta Verify on your phone, pull to refresh; use the 6-digit TOTP code instead of push
Connected but can't reach internal sites VPN tunnel up but DNS not resolving See KB-0308 (DNS) or KB-0305 (can't reach internal apps)
Menu bar icon stays grey Connection not established Click the icon > Reconnect; if it still fails, see KB-0304

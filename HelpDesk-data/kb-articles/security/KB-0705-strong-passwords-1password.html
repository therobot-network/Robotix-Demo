Create Strong Passwords and Use 1Password

Summary

Summit IT provides every employee with a 1Password Business account. Use 1Password to generate, store, and autofill strong unique passwords for every account. This article explains how to get started, how to create secure passwords, and what to do when the password manager is not available.

Why This Matters

Weak or reused passwords are one of the leading causes of account compromise. If you use the same password on a work account and a personal site that suffers a data breach, attackers can use those credentials to access Summit IT systems — a technique called credential stuffing. 1Password eliminates the need to remember passwords by generating and storing a unique, strong password for every site. You only need to remember one: your 1Password master password.

Before You Begin

  • 1Password is provisioned automatically when your Okta account is created. Check your onboarding email for your invitation.
  • If you have not received an invitation, open a ticket at https://help.summitit.com or contact the Service Desk.
  • 1Password is installed on managed Windows and macOS devices via Company Portal (Windows) or Jamf Self Service (macOS). It is also available as a browser extension and mobile app.

Steps

Part 1: Set Up 1Password

  1. Open your 1Password invitation email and click Get Started.
  2. Create a strong Master Password — this is the only password you need to remember. Use the guidelines in Part 2 below. Write it down and store it somewhere physically secure (not a sticky note on your monitor).
  3. Save your Emergency Kit PDF. This contains your Secret Key, which is required if you ever set up 1Password on a new device. Store it in a secure location outside your work device (e.g., a personal safe or secure home file).
  4. Install the 1Password desktop app and browser extension: - Windows: Open Company Portal and install 1Password. - macOS: Open Jamf Self Service and install 1Password. - Browser extension: In Chrome, Edge, or Firefox, visit https://1password.com/downloads/browser-extension and install the extension for your browser.
  5. Sign in with your Summit IT Okta credentials when prompted (single sign-on is enabled).

Part 2: Create a Strong Master Password

Your 1Password master password should be:

  • At least 16 characters long
  • A passphrase of four or more unrelated words (e.g., correct-horse-battery-staple-42) — these are easier to remember and harder to crack than short complex passwords
  • Not a phrase from a song, book, or movie
  • Not your name, your pet's name, or anything tied to your public social media

Use 1Password's built-in passphrase generator for all other accounts (see Part 3).

Part 3: Generate Strong Passwords for New Accounts

  1. When creating or updating a password on any website, click the 1Password icon in the browser address bar or the form field.
  2. Select Generate Password.
  3. Choose settings: - For most websites: Random Password, 20+ characters, with symbols and numbers enabled. - For sites with restrictions (no symbols, max 12 characters): adjust accordingly.
  4. Click Save to 1Password when prompted. The password is automatically stored.

Part 4: Autofill Saved Passwords

  1. Navigate to the login page of any saved site.
  2. Click into the username or password field. The 1Password icon will appear.
  3. Click the icon and select the correct entry. 1Password fills in both fields.
  4. On mobile, use the 1Password keyboard or share-sheet integration.

Part 5: Audit Weak or Reused Passwords

  1. Open the 1Password desktop app or go to https://my.1password.com.
  2. Click Watchtower in the left sidebar.
  3. Watchtower shows compromised, weak, or reused passwords. Click any flagged item and use Generate Password to replace it.

Part 6: Share Passwords Securely with Colleagues

  • Use 1Password Vaults to share credentials with team members — never paste passwords into Slack, Teams, or email.
  • To share a single item: right-click the item in 1Password > Share > copy the secure sharing link.
  • Shared vaults are managed by your team admin. Contact the Service Desk to request a shared vault.

Troubleshooting

  • I forgot my 1Password master password: Contact the Summit Service Desk. An admin can initiate account recovery if you have your Emergency Kit or if your account is linked to Okta SSO. Without the Emergency Kit and SSO, the vault cannot be recovered — this is by design.
  • 1Password is not autofilling on a specific site: Click the 1Password browser extension icon and search for the site manually. If the URL has changed (e.g., the site moved domains), edit the saved item and update the website field.
  • I need to access a password on a device without 1Password: Log in at https://my.1password.com using your Okta credentials. Avoid doing this on untrusted or public devices.
  • A colleague sent me a password in Slack: Immediately save it to 1Password, then ask the colleague to rotate it and share via a 1Password secure link in the future.

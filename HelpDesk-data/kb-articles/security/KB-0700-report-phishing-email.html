Report a Phishing Email with the Report Phishing Button

Summary

If you receive a suspicious email, use the built-in Report Phishing button in Outlook or the web mail client to send it directly to the Summit IT Security Operations Center (SOC). Reporting takes fewer than 10 seconds and helps protect everyone at Summit IT Services.

Why This Matters

Phishing emails are the most common way attackers steal credentials, install malware, and start ransomware incidents. Every report you submit is analyzed by the SOC team and used to block that sender or domain for all 3,200+ Summit IT users. Your report can stop an attack before it reaches someone who clicks.

Steps

In Microsoft Outlook (Windows or macOS desktop app)

  1. Open the suspicious email in the reading pane. Do not click any links or attachments.
  2. In the ribbon, click the Home tab.
  3. Click Report Phishing (located in the Protection group or the add-ins area). The button displays a shield icon with an exclamation mark.
  4. A confirmation dialog will appear: "Report this email as phishing?" Click Report.
  5. Outlook will move the email to your Deleted Items folder and send a copy to soc@summitit.com automatically.
  6. You are done. No further action is required unless the SOC contacts you.

In Outlook on the Web (https://outlook.office365.com)

  1. Select the suspicious email in your inbox. Do not open it.
  2. Click the three-dot More actions (…) menu at the top of the message list or message preview.
  3. Select Report > Report phishing.
  4. Confirm the prompt that appears.
  5. The message is flagged and forwarded to the SOC.

In Outlook Mobile (iOS / Android)

  1. Open the suspicious message.
  2. Tap the three-dot More (…) menu in the top-right corner.
  3. Tap Report Junk > Phishing.
  4. Tap Report to confirm.

If the Report Phishing Button Is Missing

  1. Do not delete the email.
  2. Forward the email as an attachment to soc@summitit.com with the subject line: Suspected Phishing – Please Review.
  3. To forward as an attachment in Outlook desktop: select the email, then go to Home > More > Forward as Attachment.

Troubleshooting

  • Button is grayed out: The email may already be in Junk or Deleted Items. Move it back to your Inbox, then report it.
  • I accidentally clicked a link inside the phishing email: Stop immediately. Disconnect from Wi-Fi if you entered credentials. Contact the SOC right now at soc@summitit.com or +1 (800) 555-7866. See also KB-0701 for immediate steps.
  • I'm not sure if it's phishing: When in doubt, report it. The SOC would rather review a legitimate marketing email than miss a real attack.
  • I already deleted it: Check Deleted Items or Trash. If it is gone, email soc@summitit.com with as many details as you remember (sender address, subject, approximate time received).

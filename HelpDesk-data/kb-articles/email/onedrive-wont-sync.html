OneDrive Won't Sync — Fix Sync Issues

Summary

OneDrive for Business automatically syncs your files between your device and the cloud. When the sync client encounters errors, a red X or yellow warning icon appears on the OneDrive icon in your system tray (Windows) or menu bar (macOS). This article covers the most common OneDrive sync problems and how to resolve them.

Before You Begin

  • Sync issues are separate from access issues. If you can access files at https://summitit-my.sharepoint.com but not on your device, the issue is with the local sync client.
  • Check your internet connection first. OneDrive requires an active internet connection to sync.
  • Note any error messages shown — hover over the OneDrive icon or click it to see the current error description.
  • If you are off-campus, connect to the GlobalProtect VPN if syncing files from a SharePoint document library that requires on-network access.

Steps

Step 1 — Check OneDrive Sync Status

Windows: 1. Look for the OneDrive cloud icon in the system tray (bottom-right taskbar area). Click it. 2. If sync is active, you will see a rotating sync icon. If paused or errored, you will see a pause or X icon. 3. Click View sync problems (if shown) or hover over files in File Explorer to see per-file sync status icons.

macOS: 1. Click the OneDrive cloud icon in the menu bar at the top of the screen. 2. The dropdown shows sync status and any recent errors. 3. Files in Finder show sync status icons (blue circle = syncing, green check = synced, red X = error).

Step 2 — Pause and Resume Sync

A simple pause/resume often resolves temporary sync stalls:

Windows: 1. Click the OneDrive icon in the system tray. 2. Click the Settings gear and select Pause syncing > 2 hours. 3. Wait 30 seconds, then click Resume syncing.

macOS: 1. Click the OneDrive icon in the menu bar. 2. Click Pause and then click Resume after 30 seconds.

Step 3 — Check for File or Folder Name Issues

OneDrive cannot sync files or folders with: - Names containing these characters: \ / : * ? " < > | - Names beginning or ending with a space or period - Names exceeding 400 characters (full path) - Files larger than 250 GB

  1. Click the OneDrive icon and look for a Files that couldn't be synced notice.
  2. Click it to open the list of problem files.
  3. Rename or move the affected files to resolve the conflict.

Step 4 — Check Available Disk Space

OneDrive needs free space on your local drive to stage downloads and temporary sync files.

Windows: Open File Explorer > This PC and check free space on your system drive (C:). macOS: Click Apple menu > About This Mac > Storage.

If disk space is critically low (under 5 GB): - Use Files On-Demand (Windows) or Online-Only (macOS) to free space without deleting files: - Windows: Right-click a large folder in OneDrive and choose Free up space. - macOS: Control-click a folder and choose Free up space.

Step 5 — Sign Out and Back In

  1. Click the OneDrive icon in the system tray or menu bar.
  2. Click Settings gear > Settings (or Preferences on macOS).
  3. Go to the Account tab and click Unlink this PC (Windows) or Sign Out (macOS).
  4. Click Unlink account to confirm.
  5. OneDrive will stop syncing. Your files already on the device remain in the OneDrive folder.
  6. Click the OneDrive icon again, or open OneDrive from the Start menu / Applications folder, and sign back in with your firstname.lastname@summitit.com credentials via Okta SSO.
  7. Choose your sync folder location (keep it as the default unless you have a specific reason to change it) and wait for sync to resume.

Step 6 — Reset the OneDrive Sync Client

If signing out and back in does not help, reset the sync client (this does not delete your files):

Windows: 1. Press Win + R, type the following, and press Enter: %localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset 2. Wait 2 minutes, then restart OneDrive from the Start menu if it does not reopen automatically.

macOS: 1. Quit OneDrive from the menu bar icon (... > Quit OneDrive). 2. Open Terminal and run: /Applications/OneDrive.app/Contents/MacOS/OneDrive --reset 3. Reopen OneDrive from your Applications folder and sign back in.

Step 7 — Check for Conflicting Files

When the same file is edited on two devices simultaneously, OneDrive creates a conflict copy. These appear in your OneDrive folder with the original file name followed by the device name and date.

  1. Look for duplicate files in your OneDrive folder (e.g., Report.docx and Report (JohnSmith-Laptop).docx).
  2. Open both versions, compare, and merge any differences.
  3. Delete the conflict copy once you have preserved all necessary content.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
"You don't have permission to sync this library" Your access to the SharePoint library may have changed. Contact the site owner or the Service Desk.
Sync icon shows "Processing changes" for more than an hour This is normal for large initial syncs (thousands of files). Leave the device on and connected. If still stuck after 24 hours, reset the client.
OneDrive icon missing from taskbar/menu bar Search for OneDrive in the Start menu (Windows) or Applications (macOS) and reopen it. Re-pin it to the taskbar if needed.
Error "The tag present in the reparse point buffer is invalid" (Windows) This is a Windows file system error. Run CHKDSK from an elevated command prompt or contact the Service Desk for L2 assistance.
Files sync on one device but not another Sign in to both devices and confirm they are using the same Microsoft 365 account.

Fix Teams Audio, Video, or Screen-Share Problems

Summary

Audio, video, and screen-sharing issues in Microsoft Teams are among the most common support requests. Most problems stem from incorrect device selection, OS permissions, or driver issues and can be resolved without IT assistance. This article covers the most effective self-service steps for Teams on Windows, macOS, and the browser.

Before You Begin

  • Test your audio and video at https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-teams/join-a-meeting — click Test your audio and video to run a pre-meeting device check without joining a call.
  • Note which device you are using: built-in laptop mic/speakers, a Jabra headset, Logitech webcam, or the Dell docking station (WD19). The standard Summit IT peripherals are Jabra headsets and Logitech cameras.
  • Check that your device is not muted at the hardware level — Jabra headsets have a physical mute button on the ear cup. A red LED on the headset means it is muted regardless of what Teams shows.
  • If your Jabra or Logitech device has a companion app (Jabra Direct, Logi Tune), keep it installed and updated — these apps push firmware updates that fix many audio/video bugs.

Steps

Audio Issues — Microphone Not Detected or No Sound

Step 1: Select the correct device in Teams 1. In Teams, click the ... (More options) next to your profile picture in the top right, then click Settings. 2. Click Devices in the left pane. 3. Under Audio devices, use the dropdown menus to select: - Speaker: Your Jabra headset or preferred output device. - Microphone: Your Jabra headset mic or built-in microphone. 4. Click Make a test call to verify audio is working.

Step 2: Check OS audio permissions

  • Windows: Go to Start > Settings > Privacy & Security > Microphone. Ensure Let apps access your microphone is On, and scroll down to confirm Microsoft Teams is allowed.
  • macOS: Go to Apple menu > System Settings > Privacy & Security > Microphone. Find Microsoft Teams in the list and toggle it On. You may need to quit and reopen Teams after granting access.

Step 3: Check physical connections - Unplug and re-plug your Jabra headset USB or 3.5mm connector. - If using a Dell WD19 dock, try connecting your headset directly to the laptop USB port to rule out dock audio passthrough issues.

Step 4: Update audio drivers (Windows) 1. Right-click the Start button and choose Device Manager. 2. Expand Audio inputs and outputs and Sound, video and game controllers. 3. Right-click each device and choose Update driver > Search automatically for drivers.

Video Issues — Camera Not Showing or Blurry/Frozen

Step 1: Select the correct camera in Teams 1. Go to Teams Settings > Devices. 2. Under Camera, select your Logitech webcam or built-in camera from the dropdown. 3. A live preview appears — confirm it shows a clear image.

Step 2: Check OS camera permissions - Windows: Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera — confirm Teams is allowed. - macOS: System Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera — confirm Microsoft Teams is enabled.

Step 3: Close other apps using the camera Only one application can use a webcam at a time. Close Zoom, FaceTime, Slack, or any other video app before joining a Teams meeting.

Step 4: Disable video background effects temporarily If the camera is detected but the video feed is frozen or laggy, background blur may be the cause on older hardware: 1. During a meeting, click the ... in the meeting toolbar > Video effects. 2. Select None to disable background blur or replacement.

Screen Sharing Issues — Others Can't See Your Screen

Step 1: Check Screen Recording / Screen Capture permissions (macOS) This is the most common cause of screen-share failure on macOS. 1. Go to Apple menu > System Settings > Privacy & Security > Screen Recording. 2. Find Microsoft Teams in the list. If it is not listed, click the + button, navigate to Applications, and add Teams. 3. Toggle Microsoft Teams On. 4. Quit and reopen Teams — you must fully quit (right-click the Dock icon > Quit) and relaunch, not just close the window.

Step 2: Share the correct window or screen 1. In a meeting, click the Share content button (rectangle with an upward arrow) in the meeting toolbar. 2. Choose between sharing your entire screen (shows everything), a window (specific app), or a PowerPoint file. 3. If sharing a video, check Include computer sound before clicking on the screen/window to share.

Step 3: Try switching to Teams in browser If the desktop app is consistently failing for screen share, try using Teams in Google Chrome at https://teams.microsoft.com. Chrome has robust screen-sharing support and can help isolate whether the issue is app-specific.

Step 4: Update Teams 1. Click your profile picture in Teams > Check for updates. 2. Teams will download and apply updates in the background. Restart Teams when prompted.

Joining a Meeting with No Audio (Web Browser)

  1. When joining a Teams meeting in the browser, click Allow when the browser asks for microphone and camera access.
  2. If you missed that prompt: click the lock icon or camera icon in the browser address bar and set Microphone and Camera to Allow.
  3. Rejoin the meeting after granting permissions.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
Echo on the call for others You likely have both speakers and microphone active without a headset. Switch to headphones or use the laptop's echo-cancellation mic.
Jabra headset muted in hardware Check for a red LED on the headset or mute button on the boom arm. Press the mute button to unmute.
No screen share option visible in meeting You may be in a guest meeting where the organizer has restricted sharing. Ask the host to enable "Allow attendees to share."
Teams says camera is in use by another app Close all other apps with camera access, or restart the device. On macOS, check Activity Monitor for lingering processes (VDCAssistant, FaceTime).
Black screen when sharing on macOS Screen Recording permission is missing for Teams — see Step 1 in Screen Sharing section above.
Low video quality in meeting Teams automatically degrades video quality on poor connections. Check your network speed at fast.com; consider switching to a wired connection.

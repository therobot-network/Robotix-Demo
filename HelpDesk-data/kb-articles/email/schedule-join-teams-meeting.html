Schedule and Join a Teams Meeting

Summary

Microsoft Teams is the standard video conferencing and collaboration platform at Summit IT Services. This article covers how to schedule a new Teams meeting from Outlook or the Teams app, how to join a meeting as an attendee, and key meeting features to know before your call.

Before You Begin

  • You must have Microsoft Teams installed on your device. On Windows, it is available through Company Portal. On macOS, install it via Self Service (Jamf).
  • Teams meetings can be scheduled from the Teams app or directly from Outlook Calendar — both methods create a standard Teams meeting link that attendees can join from any device.
  • External guests (non-Summit IT accounts) can join Teams meetings via a browser without installing Teams. They will enter a lobby and wait for a Summit IT host to admit them.
  • For audio and video setup, complete a test call first: open Teams > Settings > Devices > Make a test call.

Steps

Schedule a Teams Meeting from Outlook

  1. Open Outlook and go to your Calendar view.
  2. Click New Event (Windows) or New Meeting (macOS) in the Home tab, or double-click a time slot on the calendar.
  3. In the meeting compose window, click Teams Meeting in the ribbon (Windows) or Online Meeting > Teams Meeting (macOS). A Teams meeting link and dial-in information are automatically added to the message body.
  4. Add attendees in the To: field (type names and select from the directory).
  5. Set the meeting Title, Date, Start time, and End time.
  6. Use the Scheduling Assistant tab to check attendee availability and avoid conflicts: - Click Scheduling Assistant at the top of the compose window. - A grid shows everyone's calendar. Green blocks = free, blue/dark = busy. - Drag the blue selection bar to find a time when all attendees are available.
  7. Add an agenda or notes in the body below the Teams meeting link.
  8. Click Send.

Schedule a Teams Meeting from the Teams App

  1. Open Microsoft Teams and click the Calendar icon in the left sidebar.
  2. Click New meeting in the top-right corner.
  3. Fill in: - Title: Meeting subject - Add required attendees: Type names or email addresses. Use Optional for people who don't need to attend. - Date and time: Select start and end times. - Time zone: Confirm your time zone is correct (important when scheduling cross-timezone meetings). - Location: Leave blank for virtual-only, or add a conference room. - Details: Paste your agenda.
  4. Click Send. A calendar invite is sent to all attendees with a Teams meeting link.

Schedule a Recurring Meeting

In the Teams app or Outlook: 1. Under Repeat, select the recurrence pattern: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or Custom. 2. Set an End date or number of occurrences (leave as "No end date" only for standing meetings you intend to maintain long-term). 3. Click Send.

Add a Conference Room to Your Meeting

Summit IT offices have conference rooms bookable through Microsoft 365. 1. In the meeting compose window (Outlook or Teams), click Add a location or the Location field. 2. Start typing the conference room name (e.g., "Summit Denver - Aspen" or "Summit NYC - Hudson"). 3. Select the room from the autocomplete list. Room availability is shown in the Scheduling Assistant. 4. The room mailbox will accept or decline based on availability. You will receive a confirmation or conflict email.

Join a Teams Meeting

From your Calendar (Outlook or Teams): 1. Open the meeting on your calendar. 2. Click Join Teams Meeting in the message body or event details. 3. Teams opens and shows a pre-join screen. Select your audio and video devices, toggle your camera and microphone, and click Join now.

From the Teams App: 1. Click the Calendar icon in Teams. 2. Find the upcoming meeting and click Join. A Join button also appears in the meeting reminder that pops up 5 minutes before the meeting.

From a Meeting Link: 1. Click the Click here to join the meeting link in the calendar invite email. 2. Teams opens (or you are prompted to install it / join via browser). 3. Set up audio/video on the pre-join screen and click Join now.

From a Mobile Device: 1. Open the Outlook or Teams app on your iPhone or Android. 2. Tap the meeting in your calendar. 3. Tap Join (Teams app) or Join Teams Meeting (Outlook app). 4. Select audio (phone mic/speaker or Bluetooth headset) and tap Join now.

Joining as a Guest (External User): 1. Click the meeting link in the invitation email. 2. Choose Join on the web instead to join without installing Teams. 3. Enter your name and click Join now. You will enter the lobby — the meeting organizer must admit you.

End a Meeting for All Participants

As the meeting organizer: 1. Click the dropdown arrow next to Leave. 2. Select End meeting (not just "Leave"). This disconnects all participants at once.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
"Teams Meeting" button missing from Outlook ribbon The Teams add-in for Outlook may be disabled. Go to File > Options > Add-ins, set dropdown to "COM Add-ins," click Go, and check Microsoft Teams Meeting Add-in.
Join button not appearing in calendar Confirm the meeting was created with a Teams link. Open the invite and look for the "Click here to join" link in the body.
Cannot join — stuck in lobby indefinitely The organizer must admit you. If the meeting has ended, contact the organizer.
No audio after joining on mobile Tap the microphone icon to check if muted; check phone volume; verify Teams has microphone permission in your phone's Settings app.
Recording option not available Only organizers and presenters can record. Ask the organizer to change your role to Presenter.
External attendees cannot join Confirm the meeting was not set to "Only people in my organization can join" — the organizer can change this in Meeting Options.

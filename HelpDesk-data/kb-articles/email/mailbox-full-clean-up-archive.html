Your Mailbox Is Full — Clean Up and Archive

Summary

Every Summit IT Services mailbox includes 100 GB of primary storage plus an auto-expanding online archive. When your primary mailbox approaches its quota, Outlook will warn you and eventually stop letting you send or receive email. This article explains how to clean up your mailbox, move items to the online archive, and request additional storage if needed.

Before You Begin

  • Your current mailbox usage is visible in Outlook: go to File > Info (Windows) or hover over your name in Outlook > Preferences > Accounts (macOS).
  • Online archiving must be enabled on your account. If you do not see an Online Archive folder in Outlook, contact the Summit Service Desk to have it enabled.
  • Cleaning up does not delete items immediately from the server — the Recoverable Items folder still holds deleted content for 30 days. If you are critically close to quota, empty Deleted Items and Junk first.

Steps

Step 1 — Check Your Current Mailbox Size

Outlook on Windows: 1. Click File in the ribbon, then Info. 2. Look for the Mailbox Settings section; your usage and quota are displayed as a bar.

Outlook on the Web (https://outlook.office.com): 1. Click the Settings gear in the top right. 2. Go to General > Storage. Your primary and archive usage are shown here.

Step 2 — Empty Junk Email and Deleted Items

These folders often hold gigabytes of unneeded mail.

  1. In Outlook's left pane, right-click Junk Email and choose Empty Folder.
  2. Right-click Deleted Items (or Trash on macOS) and choose Empty Folder.
  3. Right-click Recoverable Items only if instructed to — items here count against your quota and are retained for compliance. Contact the Service Desk before purging this folder.

Step 3 — Find and Delete Large Emails

Windows: 1. Click in the Search box at the top of Outlook. 2. On the Search tab, click More > Size and set the filter to Larger than 5000 KB. 3. Sort results by Size (largest to smallest) and review — delete emails with large attachments you no longer need, especially if the file is already saved in OneDrive.

Outlook on the Web: 1. In the Search box, type size:>5MB and press Enter. 2. Sort by Size. Select and delete items you no longer need.

Step 4 — Move Old Email to the Online Archive

The Online Archive is a separate, large-quota mailbox that keeps your email searchable and accessible without consuming primary mailbox space.

  1. In Outlook (Windows or macOS), locate the Online Archive – firstname.lastname@summitit.com folder in the left pane.
  2. Drag entire folders, or select multiple messages and drag them into a folder under Online Archive.
  3. Alternatively, use AutoArchive / Archive to move items older than a set date: - Windows: Click File > Tools > Clean Up Old Items. Set a date (e.g., "Archive items older than 1 year"), choose a folder, and set the destination to your Online Archive (browse to select it). Click OK.

Step 5 — Clean Up Conversation Threads

  1. Select your Inbox or a high-volume folder.
  2. On the Home tab, click Clean Up > Clean Up Folder.
  3. Outlook will remove redundant messages in conversation threads (messages whose full text is already included in a later reply).

Step 6 — Unsubscribe from High-Volume Mailing Lists

  1. In Outlook on the Web, open a newsletter or automated email.
  2. Outlook displays an Unsubscribe banner at the top — click it to send an opt-out request automatically.
  3. For internal distribution lists, use the Summit IT self-service portal at https://help.summitit.com > Account & Access > Manage Group Memberships.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
"Online Archive" folder not visible in Outlook Contact the Service Desk to enable Online Archiving on your account.
Cannot send email even after cleanup Deleted items may still be syncing to the server. Wait 10–15 minutes, then try again.
Outlook says mailbox is full but usage looks low The Recoverable Items folder may be full. Contact the Service Desk — only admins can purge it.
Archive folder says "Cannot expand" Outlook may need to reconnect. Go to File > Account Settings, select your account, and click Repair.

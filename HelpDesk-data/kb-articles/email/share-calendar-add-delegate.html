Share Your Calendar or Add a Delegate

Summary

You can share your Outlook calendar with colleagues so they can view your availability, or grant a delegate full access to manage your calendar and email on your behalf. This article covers both scenarios: sharing calendar visibility and setting up full mailbox delegation.

Before You Begin

  • Calendar sharing lets others see your free/busy status or full event details. This is suitable for most users.
  • Delegation allows a colleague (often an assistant) to create, edit, and respond to calendar events and email on your behalf. Delegates can be given varying levels of access.
  • You can share your calendar with anyone who has a Summit IT Microsoft 365 account. Sharing outside the organization requires approval — contact the Service Desk.
  • Delegation settings changed in Outlook on Windows take up to 5 minutes to propagate.

Steps

Share Your Calendar (View-Only or Details)

Outlook on Windows: 1. Click the Calendar icon in the navigation bar at the bottom left. 2. In the Home tab, click Share Calendar. 3. In the To: field, type the name or email of the colleague you want to share with and select them from the suggestions. 4. In the Details dropdown, choose a permission level: - Availability only — shows free/busy but no event titles or details. - Limited details — shows free/busy and event titles. - Full details — shows all event information. 5. Add an optional message and click Send. Your colleague will receive an email invitation to view your calendar.

Outlook on macOS: 1. Go to the Calendar view. 2. In the left pane, right-click your calendar (under My Calendars) and choose Sharing Permissions. 3. Click the + button and search for the colleague. 4. Choose a permission level: Can view when I'm busy, Can view titles and locations, or Can view all details. 5. Click Done. An invitation is sent automatically.

Outlook on the Web: 1. Go to https://outlook.office.com and click the Calendar icon in the left sidebar. 2. Click Share at the top of the calendar view, or right-click your calendar in the left pane and choose Sharing and permissions. 3. Enter the colleague's name or email in the search box. 4. Select a permission level from the dropdown. 5. Click Share.

Add a Calendar Delegate

Delegates can create and edit events, accept or decline meeting requests, and optionally access your inbox.

Outlook on Windows: 1. Click File > Account Settings > Delegate Access. 2. Click Add… and search for the colleague you want to make a delegate. 3. Select them and click Add >, then OK. 4. In the Delegate Permissions dialog, choose permissions for each area: - Calendar: Set to Editor (can read, create, and modify items) for full calendar management. - Inbox: Set to Editor only if the delegate needs to manage your email. Leave as None otherwise. - Tasks, Contacts, Notes: Set based on your needs. 5. Check Delegate receives copies of meeting-related messages sent to me if you want the delegate to receive all meeting invitations. 6. Click OK, then OK again.

Outlook on the Web: 1. Go to Settings > View all Outlook settings > Calendar > Shared calendars. 2. Under Publish a calendar, choose your calendar and set it to Can edit. 3. Click Publish. Copy the link and share it with your delegate. 4. For full delegate access (including inbox), the Service Desk must configure this at the admin level — submit a request at https://help.summitit.com.

Access Someone Else's Calendar

Once a colleague has shared their calendar with you: 1. You will receive an email with a link — click Open this calendar to add it to your Outlook. 2. Or in Outlook, go to Calendar, click Open Calendar > Open Shared Calendar, and type the colleague's name. 3. Their calendar will appear under Other Calendars in the left pane. You can overlay it with yours by clicking the overlay arrow icon.

Remove Calendar Sharing or Delegates

Remove a shared calendar: - Windows: File > Account Settings > Delegate Access, select the delegate, and click Remove. - Web: Settings > Calendar > Shared calendars, find the share entry, and click Remove.

Remove a calendar you are viewing: - Right-click the calendar under "Other Calendars" and choose Delete Calendar.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
Colleague says they cannot see your calendar after you shared it Ask them to check the invitation email and click "Open this calendar"; it does not add automatically.
Delegate can see calendar but not respond to meeting requests In Delegate Permissions, ensure Calendar is set to Editor, not Reviewer.
Calendar sharing invitation returned as undeliverable Verify the recipient's email address is correct and that their Microsoft 365 account is active.
Delegate access not working after permissions were set Allow up to 5–10 minutes for changes to propagate; the delegate should close and reopen Outlook.

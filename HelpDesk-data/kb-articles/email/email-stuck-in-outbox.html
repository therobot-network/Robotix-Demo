Email Stuck in Outbox / Messages Won't Send

Summary

When an email gets stuck in your Outbox, Outlook keeps trying to send it but fails — often preventing other messages from going out as well. This article covers the most common causes and step-by-step fixes for Outlook on Windows, macOS, and Outlook on the Web.

Before You Begin

  • Check that you have an active internet connection. If you are off-campus, confirm GlobalProtect VPN is connected if you are trying to reach internal resources.
  • Try opening https://outlook.office.com in a browser to confirm your account is otherwise accessible. If this also fails, the issue may be account-wide — contact the Summit Service Desk.
  • Note whether the stuck message has a large attachment (over 25 MB). Microsoft 365 enforces a 25 MB per-message limit.

Steps

Step 1 — Work Offline to Release the Stuck Message

Attempting to edit or delete a stuck message while Outlook is actively trying to send it can cause errors. Working offline first prevents that.

Windows: 1. In Outlook, click the Send / Receive tab on the ribbon. 2. Click Work Offline. A checkmark appears and the status bar shows "Working Offline." 3. Go to your Outbox folder in the left pane. 4. Right-click the stuck message and choose Delete to discard it, or double-click to open and edit it. 5. If editing, make your changes and click Save (not Send) to move it to Drafts. 6. Click Send / Receive > Work Offline again to go back online. 7. Open the message from Drafts, review it, and click Send.

macOS: 1. In Outlook, go to Outlook > Work Offline from the menu bar. 2. Open the Outbox in the left sidebar. 3. Right-click the stuck message and choose Delete or Open to edit. 4. Save to Drafts if editing, then go back to Outlook > Work Offline to toggle back online. 5. Open and send the message from Drafts.

Step 2 — Check Attachment Size

If the email has an attachment larger than 25 MB: 1. Delete the stuck message from Outbox (use the Work Offline method above). 2. Upload the file to OneDrive instead: open OneDrive, drag the file in, right-click it, and choose Share > Copy Link. 3. Compose a new email and paste the OneDrive sharing link in the body.

Step 3 — Repair Your Outlook Profile (Windows)

If the message is deleted but email still won't send: 1. Close Outlook completely. 2. Open Control Panel > Mail (Microsoft Outlook) (search "Mail" in the Start menu). 3. Click Show Profiles, select your profile (usually your email address), and click Properties. 4. Click Email Accounts, select your account, and click Repair. 5. Follow the repair wizard, signing in with your Okta credentials when prompted. 6. Reopen Outlook and attempt to send a test message.

Step 4 — Use Outlook on the Web as a Workaround

While troubleshooting the desktop app, you can send email at https://outlook.office.com. Sign in with your Summit IT credentials via Okta SSO. Messages sent here are not affected by local Outlook configuration issues.

Step 5 — Clear the Outlook Cache (macOS)

  1. Quit Outlook.
  2. Open Finder > Go > Go to Folder and enter: ~/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles/
  3. Move the Main Profile folder to your Desktop as a backup.
  4. Reopen Outlook — it will rebuild the profile and prompt you to sign in via Okta.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Action
Outbox shows (1) but no message is visible The message may be corrupted. Use MFCMAPI (Windows, L2 only) or contact the Service Desk.
All new emails also get stuck Likely an authentication issue — sign out and back in to your Microsoft 365 account in Outlook settings.
Repeated "Your server does not support the connection encryption type" error Contact the Service Desk; your account's security policy may have changed.
Large attachment sent successfully in web but not desktop Desktop Outlook has a local send limit; confirm your add-ins are not blocking sends via File > Manage Add-ins.

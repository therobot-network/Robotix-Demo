Register a YubiKey Security Key

Summary

YubiKeys are FIDO2 hardware security keys required for all Summit IT employees with privileged or administrator-level access. A YubiKey generates a phishing-resistant cryptographic authentication that is stronger than a phone-based push. This article explains how to register your YubiKey in Okta once it has been issued to you by the Service Desk.

Before You Begin

  • You must have been issued a YubiKey by the Summit Service Desk. YubiKeys are not self-provisioned — open a request if you need one (see KB-0110, Request Elevated / Administrator Access).
  • Your YubiKey will have a Summit asset tag on the back (format SUM-YK-#####). Keep this for your records.
  • You need your current Summit credentials and Okta Verify MFA to authenticate before registering the key.
  • Supported connection types: USB-A, USB-C (depending on key model issued). USB-C to USB-A adapters are available from the Service Desk if your laptop only has USB-C ports (Dell Latitude 7440 has both).
  • WebAuthn registration requires a supported browser: Microsoft Edge, Chrome, or Firefox. Safari is supported on macOS 16+ only.

Steps

Part 1 — Register the YubiKey in Okta

  1. Plug your YubiKey into a USB port on your computer.
  2. Open a browser and navigate to https://summitit.okta.com.
  3. Sign in with your email and password, then approve the Okta Verify MFA push.
  4. Click your name in the top-right corner and select Settings.
  5. Scroll to Extra Verification and find the Security Key or Biometric section. Click Set up.

    If you do not see this option, your Okta admin profile may not have YubiKey enabled. Contact the Service Desk to confirm your account is configured for hardware key enrollment.

  6. Okta displays a Register Security Key dialog. Click Set up security key.
  7. Your browser displays a "Use your security key" prompt from the OS. Click OK or Allow.
  8. Touch the gold contact (the disk or button) on your YubiKey when the gold circle starts pulsing on screen. Do not press and hold — a single touch is enough.
  9. If prompted by the browser, give the key a friendly name such as YubiKey-Primary or SUM-YK-12345. Click Save.
  10. Okta confirms: "Security Key registered successfully." The key now appears in your Extra Verification list with its name and registration date.

Part 2 — Test the YubiKey

  1. Sign out of Okta (click your name → Sign out).
  2. Sign back in with your email and password.
  3. When Okta prompts for MFA, click Use security key (or select it from the factor chooser if you have multiple factors).
  4. Touch the gold contact on the YubiKey when prompted.
  5. You should reach the Okta dashboard — YubiKey authentication is working.

Part 3 — Register a Backup YubiKey (Recommended)

If you were issued two YubiKeys (standard for admin accounts), register the second one as a backup:

  1. Return to Settings → Extra Verification → Security Key or Biometric and click Set up another.
  2. Repeat Steps 6–9 with the second key, naming it YubiKey-Backup.
  3. Store the backup YubiKey in a secure location separate from your primary (e.g., your desk drawer, not your laptop bag).

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
Browser does not prompt for security key touch WebAuthn not supported in this browser Switch to Edge or Chrome; ensure browser is up to date
Key not recognized by USB port Driver issue or port is USB 2.0-only Try a different USB port; on Mac, ensure macOS is Sonoma 14 or Sequoia 15
"Security key not recognized" during sign-in Wrong key plugged in (using backup when primary is registered for this session) Try the other key; both should work if both were enrolled
Touch registering too early or not at all Touching too quickly before the pulsing indicator appears Wait for the pulsing gold circle, then touch briefly
YubiKey works on laptop but not on docking station USB passthrough issue on Dell WD19 dock Plug YubiKey directly into the laptop USB port, not the dock

What to Do If Your MFA Isn't Working or You Lost Your Phone

Summary

If you cannot approve your Okta Verify push notification — because your phone is lost, broken, replaced, or simply not working — you may be unable to sign in to Summit systems. This article covers every option available to you, from quick self-service workarounds to the emergency account recovery process.

Before You Begin

  • Work through the options below in order. Option 1 is fastest; Option 4 requires calling the Service Desk and verifying your identity.
  • If you suspect your phone was stolen along with your Summit credentials, go directly to Option 4 and inform the agent so the Security team can review your recent sign-in activity.
  • The Service Desk can perform emergency MFA resets during staffed hours (7 AM – 7 PM ET, Mon–Fri). For after-hours P1 emergencies, call the main number and follow the on-call prompt.

Steps

Option 1 — Use the TOTP Code in the Okta Verify App

If the push notification is not arriving but you still have your phone and the Okta Verify app:

  1. Open the Okta Verify app on your phone.
  2. Tap your Summit account (firstname.lastname@summitit.com).
  3. A 6-digit one-time code is displayed and refreshes every 30 seconds.
  4. On your computer's Okta sign-in screen, click Enter a code instead below the push notification waiting message.
  5. Type the 6-digit code and click Verify. You are signed in.

Common reasons push notifications fail: phone is in airplane mode, Do Not Disturb is on, the Okta Verify app needs an update, or your phone's internet connection is unstable.

Option 2 — Use Your Registered YubiKey

If you have a registered YubiKey (required for admin accounts, optional for standard accounts):

  1. Plug the YubiKey into your computer's USB port.
  2. On the Okta sign-in page, when prompted for MFA, click Use security key.
  3. Touch the gold contact on the YubiKey when the pulsing circle appears.
  4. You are signed in without needing your phone at all.

Option 3 — Use a One-Time Bypass Code (If Previously Generated)

If you generated a one-time bypass code in advance (IT recommends doing this before traveling):

  1. On the Okta MFA screen, click Enter a code instead.
  2. Enter your bypass code. These are single-use and expire after 7 days.

To generate future bypass codes while you have working MFA: 1. Sign in to Okta at https://summitit.okta.com. 2. Click your name → Settings → Extra Verification → Okta Verify → Generate a bypass code. 3. Store this code somewhere secure and separate from your phone (e.g., 1Password, printed and locked away).

Option 4 — Service Desk Emergency MFA Reset

If none of the above options are available:

  1. Contact the Summit Service Desk: - Phone (fastest): +1 (800) 555-7866 - Slack: #it-help (if you can access Slack without MFA on another device) - Email: servicedesk@summitit.com (note: email alone is not sufficient for identity verification)
  2. Tell the agent: "I am locked out due to an MFA issue and need an emergency bypass."
  3. The agent will verify your identity using one or more of the following: - Manager confirmation (agent calls your manager directly) - Video call with government-issued photo ID - Employee ID number (SUM-#####) plus answers to security questions on file
  4. Once verified, the agent issues a temporary one-time bypass code valid for 30 minutes.
  5. You use the bypass code to sign in, and then immediately re-enroll a new MFA device (see KB-0103 for moving MFA to a new device).
  6. The agent documents the reset in your ticket for the security audit trail.

Security reminder: The Service Desk will NEVER reset MFA based solely on an email. Do not respond to any email claiming to be IT and requesting that you approve a new device. Report suspicious requests to soc@summitit.com.

After Recovery — Re-Enroll Your MFA

Once you regain access, re-enroll your MFA as soon as possible: - New phone setup: see KB-0103 (Add Okta Verify to a New Phone) - First-time MFA setup: see KB-0102 (Set Up Okta Verify MFA) - Consider registering a YubiKey as a backup: see KB-0105

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
Okta Verify app shows "Account removed" Device was wiped, or app was reinstalled without re-enrollment Contact the Service Desk for an emergency reset, then re-enroll
TOTP code says "Invalid code" Clock on phone is out of sync (TOTP is time-based) On iOS: Settings → General → Date & Time → enable Set Automatically; on Android: Settings → General Management → Date and Time → enable Automatic
Push says "Approve" is greyed out Push already expired (60-second window) Click "Send push again" on the Okta page
Bypass code says "Invalid or already used" Code was already used or expired (7 days) Request a new code from Okta settings or contact the Service Desk
Service Desk cannot verify my identity Cannot reach manager; no ID available Your manager can email servicedesk@summitit.com from their Summit address to confirm your identity

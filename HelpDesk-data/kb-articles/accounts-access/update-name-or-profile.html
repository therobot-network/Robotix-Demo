Update Your Name or Profile in Okta and Microsoft 365

Summary

If your legal name has changed (e.g., due to marriage, divorce, or court order), or you want to update your preferred display name or profile photo across Summit systems, this article explains what you can update yourself and what requires a formal IT request. Legal name changes in particular must be processed by HR before IT can make the change in the system.

Before You Begin

  • Legal name changes (first name, last name, or UPN) must be initiated through HR first. IT cannot process these without an HR ticket confirmation number.
  • Display name / preferred name changes (what people see in Teams, Outlook, and Slack) can often be requested directly through IT once manager-approved.
  • Profile photos are self-service in Microsoft 365 and will sync to Teams and Outlook automatically.
  • Your Okta username (UPN) is always firstname.lastname@summitit.com. A legal last name change will result in a new UPN. Existing email aliases are preserved for 90 days after a name change.

Steps

Update Your Profile Photo (Self-Service)

Profile photos can be set yourself in Microsoft 365 and sync to Teams and Outlook within a few minutes.

  1. Go to https://myaccount.microsoft.com and sign in with your Summit credentials.
  2. Click the profile circle in the top-right corner, then click My Microsoft Account.
  3. Under your name, click the camera icon or your existing photo.
  4. Click Upload new photo, select an image from your device (JPG or PNG, recommended 200×200 px minimum, max 4 MB), and click Save.
  5. The new photo propagates to Outlook, Teams, and SharePoint within 30 minutes. In Teams specifically, sign out and back in if the photo does not update.

Update Your Preferred (Display) Name

A preferred name is the name shown in Teams, Outlook, and Slack without changing your legal records or email address.

  1. Submit a Display Name Change request at https://help.summitit.com → Service Catalog → Accounts & Access → Profile Update.
  2. Fill in: - Current Display Name (as it appears in Teams/Outlook today) - Requested Display Name (e.g., "Alex" instead of "Alexander," or a new last name) - Reason (optional, e.g., "I go by my middle name")
  3. Manager approval is required. The form sends an automated approval email to your manager.
  4. Once approved, IT updates the display name in Okta (which syncs to Microsoft Entra ID, Teams, Outlook, and Slack) within 1 business day.
  5. You receive a confirmation email when the change is live.

Legal Name Change

A legal name change updates your full name, email address, and all associated systems. Complete these steps in order.

Step 1 — Notify HR

  1. Submit your legal name change to HR with supporting documentation (court order, updated government ID, or marriage certificate as required by policy).
  2. HR updates your record in the HRIS system and issues a confirmation email with a reference number.
  3. Keep this reference number — you will need it in Step 2.

Step 2 — Submit an IT Name Change Request

  1. Go to https://help.summitit.com → Service Catalog → Accounts & Access → Legal Name Change.
  2. Fill in: - Current Legal Name (as it appears in IT systems today) - New Legal Name - HR Confirmation Number from Step 1 - Requested New Email Address (e.g., alex.johnson@summitit.com if your name changed to Alex Johnson). IT will confirm availability. - Effective Date: When you would like the change to take effect.
  3. Submit. Changes of this scope are treated as P3 and target 2 business days.

Step 4 — Update Saved Passwords and Bookmarks

  1. After your UPN changes, update any saved passwords in 1Password that reference your old email address.
  2. Inform colleagues and external contacts of your new email address — IT can configure an auto-reply on your old address for 90 days upon request.
  3. Update any services that use your Summit email as a login (e.g., vendor portals) to use your new address.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
Profile photo updated in M365 but not in Teams Teams caches profile photos In Teams, click your profile picture → Sign out → sign back in
Old name still showing in Outlook's "From" field Exchange address book cache not updated In Outlook for Windows: File → Account Settings → Download Address Book → Download Now
Sign-in fails after legal name change with new UPN Old UPN is still in browser's saved credentials Clear saved passwords for summitit.okta.com in your browser and re-authenticate
Email sent to old address bouncing 90-day forwarding alias expired or was not set up Contact IT to configure or renew the alias
Display name change reflected in Okta but not Slack Slack sync from Okta can take up to 2 hours Wait 2 hours; if still not updated, contact IT

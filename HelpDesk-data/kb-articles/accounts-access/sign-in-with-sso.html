Sign In to Summit Apps with Single Sign-On (SSO)

Summary

Summit IT uses Okta as its Single Sign-On (SSO) provider. Once you authenticate with Okta, you can launch Microsoft 365, Slack, Zoom, SharePoint, and other business applications without entering a separate username and password for each one. This article explains how SSO works and how to access your apps from the Okta dashboard or directly in your browser.

Before You Begin

  • Your Summit account must be active and your password must not be expired.
  • Okta Verify MFA must be set up on your phone (see KB-0102).
  • For applications that require VPN (internal tools, admin panels), connect to GlobalProtect VPN first.
  • Supported browsers: Microsoft Edge (recommended on Windows), Safari (recommended on macOS), Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox. Internet Explorer is not supported.

Steps

Signing In to the Okta App Dashboard

  1. Open your browser and go to https://summitit.okta.com.
  2. Enter your Summit email address (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) and click Next.
  3. Enter your password and click Sign In.
  4. Okta Verify sends a push notification to your phone. Tap Approve. - If you do not get a push, click Send push again on the Okta sign-in page, or select Enter a code instead and open the Okta Verify app to read the 6-digit TOTP code.
  5. You arrive at the Okta End User Dashboard — a grid of tiles showing every app you have been provisioned access to.
  6. Click any app tile to launch it. Okta authenticates you automatically — no second password prompt.

Launching Apps Directly (Without the Dashboard)

Most Summit apps redirect you to Okta automatically when you are not signed in:

  • Outlook Web / Microsoft 365: Go to https://office.com, click Sign in, and enter your @summitit.com address. Microsoft redirects to Okta automatically.
  • Slack: Open Slack and choose Sign in with your company email. Enter @summitit.com and you are redirected to Okta.
  • Zoom: In the Zoom app, click SSO, enter domain summitit, and you are redirected to Okta.
  • SharePoint / Teams: These are Microsoft 365 apps and follow the same flow as Outlook above.

Staying Signed In

  • Okta sessions last 8 hours on managed devices. You will be prompted to re-authenticate (password + MFA) after the session expires.
  • On personal or unmanaged devices, sessions last 4 hours and re-authentication is required more frequently.
  • Checking Keep me signed in on managed devices extends the session; this option is not available on unmanaged devices.

Accessing Apps on Mobile

  1. Download the Okta Mobile app (App Store or Google Play) for a mobile app dashboard.
  2. Sign in with your Summit credentials and approve the MFA push.
  3. Tap any app tile to launch it — most open in their native mobile app if installed, or in your phone's browser.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely Cause Fix
App tile is missing from the dashboard Access was not provisioned or was revoked Submit an access request at https://help.summitit.com
"You do not have permission to access this application" Your account is not in the required Okta group Contact your manager to request access, or open a ticket
Redirect loop between app and Okta Browser cookies or cache corrupted Clear browser cookies for okta.com and the app domain, then try again
MFA push not arriving Phone offline, Do Not Disturb enabled Use the TOTP code in Okta Verify app instead
SSO works in browser but not in desktop app (Outlook, Teams) App is signed in with a personal Microsoft account Sign out of the desktop app, then sign in again with @summitit.com address
"Your session has expired" immediately after login System clock is wrong by more than 2 minutes Sync your device clock: Windows — Settings → Time → Sync Now; macOS — System Settings → General → Date & Time → enable automatic

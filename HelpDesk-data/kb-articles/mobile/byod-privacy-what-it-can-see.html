Understand What IT Can and Can't See on Your BYOD Phone

Summary

If you enroll your personal phone in Summit IT's Intune MDM to access work email and apps, you may have questions about what IT can monitor on your device. This article explains clearly what Summit IT can and cannot see when managing a BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) phone.

Before You Begin

  • This article applies only to personal BYOD devices enrolled in Intune. Company-issued devices have broader management capabilities.
  • Summit IT uses Microsoft Intune App Protection Policies (APP) and Android Work Profiles / iOS Managed Apps to isolate work data from personal data.
  • This information reflects Summit IT's current MDM configuration. If you have questions about the legal or HR policy context, refer to the Summit IT Acceptable Use Policy available on the internal SharePoint intranet.

Steps

There are no steps to complete in this article — it is an informational reference.

What IT Can See on Your BYOD Device

Category What IT can see
Device inventory Device make, model, OS version, and serial number
Compliance status Whether the device meets Summit's security requirements (PIN set, OS up to date, not jailbroken/rooted)
Enrolled apps The list of Summit-managed apps installed through Company Portal (e.g., Outlook, Teams)
Work profile activity (Android) App inventory within the work profile only
Device health Whether device encryption is enabled; CrowdStrike sensor status (if installed)
Remote actions Ability to remotely lock the device or issue a selective wipe of Summit work data

What IT Cannot See on Your BYOD Device

Category What IT cannot see
Personal apps Apps installed outside the work profile or Company Portal are not visible to IT
Personal photos and videos Photos stored in your personal camera roll are never accessible to IT
Personal email and messages iMessages, personal Gmail, WhatsApp, and other personal communications are private
Browser history Your personal browsing activity (Safari, Chrome personal profile) is not monitored
Location Summit IT does not track your GPS location on BYOD devices. Location tracking is only available on company-issued devices and only when the device is reported lost or stolen
Personal files Documents, notes, and files outside the Intune-managed containers are inaccessible
Call and SMS history Personal call logs and text messages are not visible

How Work and Personal Data Are Separated

Android Work Profile

Android creates a completely isolated container called the work profile for all Summit-managed apps. Apps inside the work profile (marked with a briefcase icon) cannot share data with personal apps, and vice versa. IT has visibility and control only within the work profile. Your personal apps, including those outside the work profile, are invisible to IT.

iOS Managed Apps

On iOS, Intune uses Managed Apps (apps distributed through Company Portal) and App Protection Policies. These policies prevent copying data from managed apps (like Outlook) to unmanaged personal apps (like personal Notes), and prevent attaching files from personal storage to managed apps. IT cannot see your personal apps, messages, or data — only the behavior and status of the managed apps.

What Happens When You Unenroll or Leave Summit IT

  • Voluntary unenrollment: Open Company Portal, tap your device, and tap Remove Device. IT will issue a selective wipe — all Summit apps and their data (email, Teams messages, cached files) are removed. Your personal data remains untouched.
  • Offboarding: When your Summit account is deactivated, IT will issue a selective wipe within 24 hours. Personal data is not affected.
  • Full device wipe: Summit IT does not remotely perform a full factory reset on BYOD devices. Full wipes are reserved for company-issued devices only.

Troubleshooting

Question Answer
Can IT read my personal texts? No. Personal SMS and messaging apps are outside Intune's management scope.
Can IT see my location right now? No. BYOD location tracking is disabled in Summit IT's Intune configuration.
Will IT wipe my whole phone if I'm let go? No. Only Summit corporate data is removed via selective wipe. Personal data remains on the device.
Can I opt out of MDM and still get work email? No. Intune enrollment is required to access Summit email and apps on a personal device per the Acceptable Use Policy.
Who can I contact with privacy concerns? Contact the Service Desk or your HR Business Partner. You can also review the IT Acceptable Use Policy on the Summit intranet.

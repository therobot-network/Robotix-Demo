Move Okta Verify to a New Phone

Summary

Okta Verify is Summit IT's primary multi-factor authentication (MFA) app. When you get a new phone — whether upgrading or replacing a lost device — you need to migrate your Okta Verify account to the new device before your old phone is wiped or loses service. This article covers both planned transfers and emergency recovery scenarios.

Before You Begin

  • If you still have access to your old phone, the transfer is straightforward and takes about 5 minutes.
  • If you no longer have access to your old phone (lost, stolen, or already wiped), you will need to verify your identity with the Service Desk before IT can reset your MFA enrollment.
  • You need access to your Summit password. If you have forgotten it, contact the Service Desk first.
  • The Okta Verify app is free and available on the App Store (iOS) and Google Play (Android).

Steps

Option A — Planned Transfer (Old Phone Still Available)

  1. On your new phone, download Okta Verify from the App Store or Google Play.
  2. On your old phone, open Okta Verify, tap your Summit IT account, and tap Move accounts to new device.
  3. Okta Verify on your old phone displays a QR code and prompts you to scan it with your new phone.
  4. On your new phone, open Okta Verify, tap Get Started (or the + icon), and select Scan a QR code.
  5. Scan the QR code displayed on your old phone. Your Summit account transfers to the new device.
  6. Okta sends a push notification to your new phone — tap Approve to verify the transfer succeeded.
  7. Sign in to https://okta.summitit.com from a browser to confirm MFA works on the new device.
  8. Once verified, remove the account from your old phone by opening Okta Verify on the old phone, tapping your account, and selecting Remove Account.

Option B — Emergency Recovery (Old Phone Not Available)

  1. Call the Summit Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866 and state that you need an MFA reset.
  2. The agent will verify your identity using your employee ID (SUM-#####) and one additional verification factor (usually your Summit email sent to a recovery address on file or a manager confirmation).
  3. IT will delete your current Okta Verify enrollment, which temporarily disables MFA.
  4. You will receive an email to your Summit address with an MFA re-enrollment link (valid for 24 hours).
  5. On your new phone, install Okta Verify from the App Store or Google Play.
  6. Open the enrollment link on your new phone (or scan the QR code in the email).
  7. Tap Set Up and follow the prompts in Okta Verify. You will be asked to approve a test push notification.
  8. Enrollment is complete. Sign in to https://okta.summitit.com to confirm.

Re-enroll Your New Phone in Intune (If Applicable)

After completing the Okta Verify transfer, if your new phone needs access to Summit email and apps, complete the Intune enrollment process: see KB-0605.

Troubleshooting

Symptom What to try
"Account already exists" when scanning QR code on new phone Remove the Summit account from the new phone in Okta Verify, then retry the scan.
QR code on old phone expired Refresh the QR code by tapping Regenerate on the old phone's Okta Verify transfer screen.
Push notification not arriving on new phone Ensure Okta Verify has notification permissions enabled: iOS: Settings > Okta Verify > Notifications; Android: Settings > Apps > Okta Verify > Notifications.
"Your account has been locked" error Three failed MFA attempts trigger a temporary lockout. Wait 15 minutes or contact the Service Desk to unlock.
Service Desk says identity cannot be verified by phone Visit your local IT office in person with your Summit employee badge for in-person identity verification.

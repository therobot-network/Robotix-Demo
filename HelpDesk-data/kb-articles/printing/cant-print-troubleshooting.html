Can't Print — Printer Troubleshooting

Summary

If your print job does not reach the printer, stays stuck in the queue, or produces an error, use this guide to diagnose and resolve the most common printing problems on Summit-managed HP and Xerox MFPs with PaperCut.

Before You Begin

  • Confirm the printer is powered on and its front panel does not show a critical error (red indicator light or error message on screen).
  • Check that you are connected to Summit-Corp Wi-Fi or GlobalProtect VPN — printing is unavailable on Summit-Guest or off-network without VPN.
  • Know the printer's name or location (displayed on the label on the device).

Steps

Step 1 — Check the print queue on your computer

Windows 11: 1. Open Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners. 2. Click the printer you used. 3. Click Open print queue. 4. If a job shows Error or Deleting, right-click it and choose Cancel. Then right-click the same job and choose Restart if the option is available.

macOS: 1. Open System Settings > Printers & Scanners. 2. Click the printer, then click Open Print Queue… 3. If a job is paused or shows an error icon, click the Resume button or delete the job and reprint.

Step 2 — Restart the PaperCut client

Windows 11: 1. Right-click the PaperCut icon in the system tray. 2. Select About and confirm the client shows Connected. 3. If it shows Disconnected, right-click and choose Restart PaperCut Client, then try printing again.

macOS: 1. Click the PaperCut menu bar icon. 2. If status reads Disconnected, select Quit PaperCut Client. 3. Reopen PaperCut from Applications > PaperCut or Launchpad.

Step 3 — Verify the correct print queue is selected

  1. When printing, open the Printer dropdown in the print dialog.
  2. Select the queue named Summit-FollowMe for Follow-Me secure printing, or select the named printer (e.g., Denver-Floor3-HP-MFP) for direct printing.
  3. Avoid using queues labelled (offline) — these indicate the printer is unreachable.

Step 4 — Restart the printer (if you have physical access)

  1. Press and hold the power button on the printer's front panel for 5 seconds.
  2. Wait for the printer to fully power off (all lights off).
  3. Press the power button again and wait up to 90 seconds for the printer to boot.
  4. Retry your print job.

Step 5 — Reinstall the printer on your computer

If none of the above resolves the issue:

Windows 11: 1. Open Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners. 2. Click the printer and select Remove. 3. Use PaperCut (system tray > Find a Printer) to re-add the printer. See KB-0600 for full steps.

macOS: 1. Open System Settings > Printers & Scanners. 2. Select the printer and click the – (minus) button to remove it. 3. Re-add via the PaperCut menu bar icon.

Troubleshooting

Symptom Likely cause Fix
Queue is stuck and jobs won't cancel Print spooler issue Windows: Run services.msc, find Print Spooler, right-click > Restart. macOS: Run sudo launchctl stop org.cups.cupsd in Terminal.
"Driver unavailable" error Corrupt or missing driver Remove the printer and re-add via PaperCut; the correct HP/Xerox driver installs automatically.
Print job disappears but nothing prints PaperCut held-job timeout Job may have expired (4-hour limit). Reprint and release at the MFP promptly.
Printer shows "Offline" in Windows Network path changed Remove and re-add the printer. Confirm you are on Summit-Corp Wi-Fi or VPN.
Job prints to wrong printer Wrong queue selected Check the Printer dropdown; select the correct named queue or Follow-Me.
Pages come out blank Toner low or unseated Check printer front panel; see KB-0604 to report supply issues.

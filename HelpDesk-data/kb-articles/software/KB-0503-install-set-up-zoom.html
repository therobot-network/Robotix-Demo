Install and Set Up Zoom

Summary

Zoom is Summit IT's standard video-conferencing platform for external meetings, large all-hands sessions, and webinars. For internal collaboration, Slack huddles and Microsoft Teams are also available; however, Zoom is the preferred tool when meeting with clients or partners outside the organization. This article covers installing Zoom from the approved catalog and signing in with your Summit IT account using SSO.

Before You Begin

  • Do not download Zoom from zoom.us directly. Install it through Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (Mac) to ensure you receive the IT-managed version with correct settings.
  • Your Summit IT account includes a Zoom Pro license. You do not need a personal Zoom account.
  • Sign in to Zoom using SSO (Single Sign-On) with your Okta credentials — do not create a separate Zoom password.
  • A working webcam and microphone are required for video calls. All standard-issue Dell Latitude laptops and MacBook Pros have built-in cameras and microphones.

Steps

Install Zoom

Windows: 1. Open Company Portal from the Start menu. 2. Click Apps in the left navigation pane. 3. Search for Zoom and click the result labeled Zoom Workplace. 4. Click Install and wait for the status to change to Installed (typically 2–4 minutes).

macOS: 1. Open Self Service from your Applications folder or Dock. 2. Search for Zoom using the search bar in the top-right corner. 3. Click Install next to Zoom Workplace. The app will install silently.

Sign In to Zoom

  1. Open Zoom from the Start menu (Windows) or Applications folder (Mac).
  2. On the sign-in screen, click Sign In with SSO — do not click "Sign In" or "Sign Up".
  3. In the Company Domain field, enter summitit and click Continue.
  4. Your browser will open and redirect to the Okta login page. Enter your Summit IT credentials and approve the Okta Verify push notification.
  5. Click Open Zoom (or Allow) when the browser prompts you to return to the Zoom app.
  6. Zoom will open and display your name and profile picture. You are now signed in.

Configure Audio and Video Settings

  1. In Zoom, click your profile picture in the top-right corner, then click Settings.
  2. Click Audio in the left sidebar: - Under Microphone, select your preferred input device (e.g., "Built-in Microphone" or your Jabra headset). - Under Speaker, select your preferred output device. - Click Test Speaker and Test Mic to confirm both work correctly.
  3. Click Video in the left sidebar: - Select your camera from the dropdown menu. - Enable HD if available and your connection supports it. - Check Touch up my appearance if desired (applies a subtle filter).
  4. Click General and enable Start Zoom when I start my computer if you want Zoom available immediately at login.
  5. Close Settings. Your preferences are saved automatically.

Add Zoom to Outlook (Optional)

Zoom integrates with Outlook to add meeting links to calendar invites automatically.

  1. Open Outlook on Windows or Mac.
  2. Go to the Get Add-ins option (Windows: Home > Get Add-ins; Mac: Home > Add-ins).
  3. Search for Zoom for Outlook and click Add.
  4. A Zoom button will appear in the Outlook ribbon. When creating a meeting, click Add a Zoom Meeting to insert a Zoom link automatically.

Troubleshooting

Zoom opens but shows "Your license does not allow you to start this meeting." Your Zoom account may not yet be provisioned. Wait up to 30 minutes after your first SSO sign-in for the license to apply. If the issue persists after an hour, contact the Service Desk.

No audio during a Zoom call. Click the ^ arrow next to the microphone icon in the Zoom call toolbar and verify the correct microphone and speaker are selected. If your Jabra headset is not listed, disconnect and reconnect the USB cable, then re-check the dropdown.

Camera shows a black screen. On macOS, go to System Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera and ensure Zoom is toggled on. On Windows, go to Settings > Privacy & security > Camera and enable access for Zoom.

Zoom asks me to sign in every time I open it. In Zoom Settings > General, check Keep me signed in. If you were signed in with a personal account previously, sign out completely, then sign in again using SSO as described above.

"Can't connect to our service. Please check your network connection." Connect to the GlobalProtect VPN if you are off-site, or switch from Wi-Fi to a wired connection. If on Summit-Corp Wi-Fi and still seeing this error, contact the Service Desk.

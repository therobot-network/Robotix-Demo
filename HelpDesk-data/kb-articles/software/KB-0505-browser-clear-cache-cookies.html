Browser Problems — Clear Cache and Cookies

Summary

Clearing your browser's cache and cookies resolves a wide range of web-related issues, including pages that fail to load, login loops, outdated content, and broken layouts in internal tools like https://help.summitit.com. Summit IT supports Microsoft Edge (default on Windows), Google Chrome, and Safari (macOS only). The steps below cover all three browsers.

Before You Begin

  • Clearing cookies will sign you out of most websites and web apps, including Microsoft 365, Okta, Slack (web), and https://help.summitit.com. Have your Summit IT credentials (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) and Okta Verify ready before you proceed.
  • You do not need administrator rights to clear browser data.
  • If you only want to test whether the cache is the issue, try opening the affected site in a private / incognito window first. If the site works there, cache or cookies are likely the cause.
  • Clearing cache and cookies does not delete saved passwords in 1Password — those are stored in the 1Password extension, not the browser.

Steps

Microsoft Edge (Windows — Default Browser)

  1. Open Microsoft Edge.
  2. Click the Settings and more icon (three horizontal dots ···) in the top-right corner, or press Ctrl + Shift + Delete.
  3. Click Settings > Privacy, search, and services.
  4. Under the Clear browsing data section, click Choose what to clear.
  5. Set the Time range to All time.
  6. Check the boxes for: - Browsing history (optional, but recommended) - Cookies and other site data - Cached images and files
  7. Click Clear now.
  8. Close and reopen Edge, then try the affected site again.

Google Chrome (Windows and macOS)

  1. Open Google Chrome.
  2. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete (Windows) or Command + Shift + Delete (Mac). The Clear browsing data dialog will open.
  3. Click the Advanced tab.
  4. Set the Time range to All time.
  5. Check the boxes for: - Browsing history (optional) - Cookies and other site data - Cached images and files
  6. Click Clear data.
  7. Restart Chrome and revisit the affected site.

Safari (macOS Only)

  1. Open Safari.
  2. In the menu bar, click Safari > Settings (macOS Sonoma/Sequoia) or Preferences (older versions).
  3. Click the Privacy tab.
  4. Click Manage Website Data…, then click Remove All. Confirm by clicking Remove Now.
  5. To also clear the cache, return to the menu bar and click Develop > Empty Caches. (If you do not see the Develop menu, go to Safari Settings > Advanced and check Show features for web developers.)
  6. Restart Safari and test the affected site.

Hard Refresh (Quick Fix — No Data Deleted)

Before clearing all cache and cookies, try a hard refresh to force the browser to reload the page from the server: - Windows (Edge / Chrome): Press Ctrl + Shift + R or Ctrl + F5. - macOS (Chrome / Safari): Press Command + Shift + R.

Troubleshooting

Clearing cache did not fix the problem. If the issue persists after clearing cache and cookies, try a different browser. If the site works in Edge but not Chrome (or vice versa), the problem may be a browser extension. Try disabling extensions one by one (Edge: edge://extensions/; Chrome: chrome://extensions/) to identify the culprit.

I am stuck in a login loop on https://help.summitit.com or an Okta-protected app. Clear cookies specifically for summitit.okta.com and the affected site domain, then sign out of all Okta sessions at https://summitit.okta.com, close all browser windows, and sign in fresh.

The page looks broken after clearing cache (missing styles or images). Wait 30 seconds and press Ctrl + Shift + R (Windows) or Command + Shift + R (Mac) to force a full reload. Some pages take a moment to regenerate cached assets.

I accidentally cleared my saved passwords. Passwords are stored in 1Password, not the browser. Open the 1Password extension or desktop app to retrieve your credentials. If you were using the browser's built-in password manager, contact the Service Desk — browser-saved passwords are not backed up by IT.

The problem only occurs on one specific website, not others. The issue may be on the website's server side, not your browser. Check if colleagues can access the same site. If it is an external site, check the vendor's status page. If it is an internal tool, contact the Service Desk.

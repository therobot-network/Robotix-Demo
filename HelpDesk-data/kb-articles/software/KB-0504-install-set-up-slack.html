Install and Set Up Slack

Summary

Slack is Summit IT's primary internal messaging and collaboration platform. All company announcements, team channels, and the IT help channel (#it-help) live in Slack. This article covers installing the IT-managed Slack desktop app and signing in to the Summit IT workspace using your Okta credentials.

Before You Begin

  • Install Slack through Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (Mac). Do not download Slack from slack.com, as the IT-managed version includes required security settings and the correct workspace URL pre-configured.
  • Your Summit IT account is automatically provisioned in Slack. You do not need to create a separate Slack account or accept an invitation email before following these steps.
  • Sign in using Sign In with Okta (SAML SSO) — do not set a standalone Slack password.
  • The Summit IT Slack workspace URL is summitit.slack.com.

Steps

Install Slack

Windows: 1. Open Company Portal from the Start menu. 2. Click Apps in the left navigation pane and search for Slack. 3. Click Slack in the results and then click Install. 4. Wait for the status to change to Installed (typically 1–3 minutes).

macOS: 1. Open Self Service from your Applications folder or Dock. 2. Search for Slack in the top-right search bar. 3. Click Install. The app installs silently; when the button label changes to Reinstall, Slack is ready.

Sign In to the Summit IT Workspace

  1. Open Slack from the Start menu (Windows) or Applications folder (Mac).
  2. On the welcome screen, click Sign In to Slack.
  3. In the workspace URL field, type summitit and click Continue (or the full address summitit.slack.com).
  4. Click Sign In with Okta. Your default browser will open the Okta login page.
  5. Enter your Summit IT credentials (firstname.lastname@summitit.com) and approve the Okta Verify push notification.
  6. When the browser displays "You can close this tab," switch back to the Slack desktop app. You should now be signed in and see the Summit IT workspace.

Recommended First Steps After Signing In

  1. Set your profile: Click your name in the top-left corner > Profile > Edit. Add your job title, phone number, and a photo. This helps teammates identify you.
  2. Join relevant channels: Click the + icon next to Channels in the left sidebar and search for your team's channels (e.g., #engineering, #marketing, #denver-office). Click Join.
  3. Configure notifications: Go to Slack menu > Preferences > Notifications. Set your notification schedule to match your working hours to avoid after-hours pings.
  4. Set a status: Click your profile picture > Update your status to let teammates know when you are in a meeting, out of office, or focused.
  5. Connect to IT help: Join #it-help — the fastest way to reach the Summit Service Desk for non-urgent questions.

Set Up Slack on Mobile (Optional)

  1. On your personal or company phone, download Slack from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android).
  2. Open Slack and sign in with summitit.slack.com as the workspace URL.
  3. Tap Sign In with Okta and authenticate using Okta Verify on your device.

Troubleshooting

Slack shows "Your workspace URL is not valid." Verify you typed summitit (no spaces, no extra characters) and try again. If you are using the full URL, confirm it is summitit.slack.com.

"You're not authorized to access this workspace." Your Okta account may not yet have the Slack application assigned. This sometimes occurs on the first day of onboarding. Contact the Service Desk and ask them to verify your Slack provisioning in Okta.

Notifications are not appearing on Windows. Go to Windows Settings > System > Notifications. Scroll to the Slack entry and ensure notifications are enabled. In Slack Preferences > Notifications, confirm your notification schedule includes the current time.

Notifications are not appearing on macOS. Go to System Settings > Notifications > Slack and ensure Allow Notifications is toggled on. Also check that Focus mode is not silencing apps.

Slack keeps signing me out. This can be caused by a session policy enforced by IT. Re-sign in using SSO. If you are signed out multiple times per day, contact the Service Desk — it may indicate a device compliance issue in Okta.

Messages are not syncing / Slack is very slow. Quit Slack completely (right-click the taskbar icon and choose Quit on Windows; Slack > Quit Slack on Mac) and reopen it. If slowness persists, clear the Slack cache: go to Help > Troubleshooting > Clear Cache and Restart (Windows) or Slack > Troubleshooting > Clear Cache and Restart (Mac).

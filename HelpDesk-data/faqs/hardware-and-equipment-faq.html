Hardware & Equipment FAQ

Common questions about Summit IT-issued laptops, monitors, peripherals, docking stations, and how to request or replace equipment.

What hardware does Summit IT issue to employees?

Standard Summit IT equipment includes a Dell Latitude 7440 (Windows) or MacBook Pro 14" with Apple silicon (Mac), a Dell 24" or 27" USB-C monitor, a Logitech keyboard and mouse, a Jabra or Logitech headset, and a Dell WD19 or USB-C docking station. The specific model you receive depends on your role and whether your manager requested Windows or Mac during onboarding.

How do I find my laptop's asset tag?

Your asset tag is a sticker on the bottom of your laptop in the format SUM-LT-##### (Dell Latitude) or SUM-MB-##### (MacBook). You will need this when submitting support tickets or reporting a lost or stolen device. It is also registered in SummitDesk under your name — you can look it up at https://help.summitit.com under My Assets.

My laptop won't turn on — what should I do first?

Ensure the power adapter is firmly connected to both the laptop and the wall outlet. If using a docking station, try plugging the power adapter directly into the laptop instead. Hold the power button for 5–7 seconds. If there is still no response, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com or call +1 (800) 555-7866 — a technician will assess whether the issue is hardware or power-related.

How do I connect my laptop to a monitor and docking station?

Connect the Dell WD19 or USB-C dock to your laptop using the included USB-C or Thunderbolt cable. Connect your monitor to the dock using the DisplayPort or HDMI port on the dock. Plug the dock's power adapter into the wall. The dock will charge your laptop and pass video signal simultaneously. For Mac users, ensure your MacBook Pro display settings are configured correctly under System Settings > Displays.

My monitor is not displaying anything — what should I check?

First, confirm the monitor is powered on (look for the power LED). Check that the cable between the dock/laptop and monitor is firmly seated at both ends. Try a different cable or port if available. On Windows, press Win + P to cycle display modes (mirror, extend, second screen only). On Mac, go to System Settings > Displays and click Detect Displays. If none of these work, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com.

How do I request additional or replacement equipment?

Submit a service request at https://help.summitit.com under the Hardware > Equipment Request category. Specify what you need (e.g., additional monitor, replacement keyboard), your business justification, and your office location or shipping address if remote. Manager approval is required for most hardware requests and will be routed automatically through SummitDesk.

My laptop screen is cracked or physically damaged — what do I do?

Report the damage immediately by submitting a ticket at https://help.summitit.com or calling +1 (800) 555-7866. Do not attempt to repair or open the laptop yourself. IT will assess whether the damage is covered under warranty and arrange repair or replacement. If the damage was caused by an accident, you may be asked to complete an incident report.

My keyboard or mouse is not working — what should I try first?

For wireless Logitech keyboards and mice, check that the USB receiver (Unifying Receiver) is securely plugged in and try a different USB port. Replace the batteries if the device is battery-powered, or charge it via USB if it is rechargeable. Try restarting your laptop. If the device still does not work, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com for a replacement to be sent.

Can I use personal peripherals (keyboard, mouse, headset) with my work laptop?

Yes, personal peripherals are permitted for productivity. However, they are not supported by IT and do not come with warranty replacements. Do not connect personal USB storage devices (flash drives, external hard drives) to your work laptop without prior approval from IT, as this may violate data security policy.

How do I install approved software on my laptop?

On Windows (Dell Latitude): open Company Portal from the Start menu, browse or search for the application, and click Install. On Mac (MacBook Pro): open Self Service from the Applications folder, find the app, and click Install. Both are managed by IT (Intune and Jamf Pro respectively) and install software silently without administrator credentials.

My laptop is running very slowly — what are the first steps I should take?

Restart your laptop first, as this clears memory and applies any pending updates. Close any applications you are not actively using. Check whether any system updates are pending (Windows Update or macOS Software Update) and allow them to complete. If the laptop remains slow after a fresh restart and updates, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com for a performance investigation.

What should I do if my laptop is lost or stolen?

Report it immediately to the Service Desk by calling +1 (800) 555-7866) — this is treated as a P1 security incident. The security team will remotely wipe the device using Intune (Windows) or Jamf (Mac). Also file a police report if the device was stolen, and provide the report number to IT. Have your asset tag (SUM-LT-##### or SUM-MB-#####) ready if available.

How do I return equipment when I leave the company or transfer roles?

IT will contact you during the offboarding or transition process with return instructions. Typically, you will ship the laptop and accessories back to IT using a prepaid label, or drop them off at your local Summit IT office. Ensure all personal files are removed from company devices before return — the device will be wiped upon receipt.

Can I take my work laptop home or travel with it?

Yes. Summit IT laptops are intended for both office and remote use. When working remotely, connect to GlobalProtect VPN to access internal resources. When traveling internationally, check with the Service Desk at servicedesk@summitit.com in advance — some countries have import/export restrictions on encryption hardware that may affect your laptop.

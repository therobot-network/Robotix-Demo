General IT Help Desk FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about how Summit IT's Service Desk works, what we support, and how to get help fast.

How do I contact the IT Service Desk?

You can reach us through four channels: submit a ticket at the self-service portal https://help.summitit.com, email servicedesk@summitit.com, call +1 (800) 555-7866, or message us in Slack at #it-help. For security incidents or suspected phishing, email soc@summitit.com or use the Report Phishing button in Outlook.

What are the IT Service Desk hours?

The Service Desk is staffed Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM ET. After-hours on-call support is available 24/7 for P1 (Critical) incidents only — call +1 (800) 555-7866 and follow the prompts.

What is SummitDesk?

SummitDesk is Summit IT's IT service management (ITSM) platform, accessible at https://help.summitit.com. You can use it to submit incident tickets, make service requests, check ticket status, browse the knowledge base, and approve change requests.

How do I submit a support ticket?

Log in to https://help.summitit.com with your Okta credentials, click New Request, select the appropriate category, fill in the description, and submit. You will receive an email confirmation with a ticket ID in the format INC-###### (incident) or REQ-###### (service request).

How do I check the status of my ticket?

Log in to https://help.summitit.com and navigate to My Requests to view open and recently closed tickets, current status, and any comments from the assigned technician.

What are the support priority levels and response times?

Summit IT uses a four-tier priority system: - P1 – Critical: Major outage affecting many users — 15-minute response, 4-hour resolution target. - P2 – High: Significant impact on a single critical user — 1-hour response, 8 business-hour resolution. - P3 – Medium: Standard issue with a workaround available — 4-hour response, 2 business-day resolution. - P4 – Low: Minor requests or enhancements — 1 business-day response, 5 business-day resolution.

Who sets the priority on my ticket?

The Service Desk agent who triages your ticket assigns the priority based on business impact and the number of users affected. If you believe the priority is incorrect, you can request an escalation by replying to your ticket or calling +1 (800) 555-7866.

What does the IT Service Desk support?

The Service Desk supports all standard hardware (Dell Latitude, MacBook Pro, Dell monitors, docking stations), software in the Summit IT stack (Microsoft 365, Okta, GlobalProtect VPN, CrowdStrike, 1Password, Zoom, Slack), network connectivity (Summit-Corp Wi-Fi, VPN), and printer/peripheral issues. Personal devices and non-approved applications are out of scope.

What is L1, L2, and L3 support?

L1 (Service Desk) handles first contact, resets, and how-to questions. L2 (Desktop & Systems) resolves endpoint, account, and application issues. L3 (Engineering & Infrastructure) handles complex network, server, identity, and cloud issues. Vendor escalations (Microsoft, Okta, Palo Alto, etc.) are arranged by L3 when needed.

Can I request software to be installed on my computer?

Yes. Submit a software request via https://help.summitit.com. Windows users can install approved applications through Company Portal (managed by Microsoft Intune). Mac users can install approved applications via Self Service (managed by Jamf Pro). Requests for non-catalogue software require manager approval and a security review before installation.

What should I do if I think I've been hacked or my credentials are compromised?

Contact the Service Desk immediately by calling +1 (800) 555-7866 or emailing soc@summitit.com. Change your Okta password right away at https://summit.okta.com and do not use the compromised device until cleared by the security team. This will be treated as a P1 or P2 incident.

What is the difference between an incident and a service request?

An incident (INC-######) is an unplanned interruption to a service — something broken that needs fixing. A service request (REQ-######) is a standard, planned request for something new — like a software install, equipment order, or access grant. Both are submitted through https://help.summitit.com.

How do I escalate a ticket if I'm not getting timely updates?

Reply directly to the ticket notification email or add a comment in SummitDesk asking for an update. If you need urgent escalation, call +1 (800) 555-7866 and reference your ticket ID. For Slack, post in #it-help with your ticket number and a brief description of the urgency.

Can I add someone else to my ticket (e.g., my manager)?

Yes. Open your ticket in SummitDesk at https://help.summitit.com and use the Add Watcher feature to include additional email addresses. Those people will receive all ticket update notifications.

What information should I include when submitting a ticket?

Include your full name, employee ID (format SUM-#####), office location or "Remote," your device asset tag (format SUM-LT-##### for laptops or SUM-MB-##### for Macs), a clear description of the issue, and any error messages or screenshots. The more detail you provide upfront, the faster we can resolve your issue.

How do I provide feedback on a closed ticket?

After a ticket is closed, you will receive a satisfaction survey via email. Responses go directly to the Service Desk management team and are used to improve service quality. You can also email servicedesk@summitit.com with feedback at any time.

Passwords & Login FAQ

Common questions about logging in, resetting passwords, MFA, and managing credentials at Summit IT.

How do I reset my Okta password?

Go to https://summit.okta.com and click Forgot password? on the login page. You will be prompted to verify your identity via Okta Verify push notification or a code sent to your recovery email/phone. If you are completely locked out and cannot receive a push, call the Service Desk at +1 (800) 555-7866 for an assisted reset.

What are the password requirements at Summit IT?

Okta passwords must be at least 14 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Passwords expire every 90 days — you will receive reminder notifications via email starting 14 days before expiry. Previously used passwords (last 10) cannot be reused.

My account is locked — what do I do?

Accounts lock automatically after 5 consecutive failed login attempts. Wait 15 minutes and try again, or click Forgot password? at https://summit.okta.com to unlock via self-service. If self-service is unavailable, call +1 (800) 555-7866 or message #it-help in Slack — a Service Desk agent can unlock your account after verifying your identity.

How do I change my password (not reset — I know my current password)?

Log in to your Okta dashboard at https://summit.okta.com, click your profile icon in the top-right corner, select Settings, and then Change Password. Enter your current password, then your new password twice. The change takes effect immediately across all Okta SSO-connected applications.

What is Okta Verify and what do I do if I lose access to it?

Okta Verify is the push-based MFA app installed on your enrolled smartphone. If you lose your phone, get a new device, or delete the app, you will need the Service Desk to reset your MFA enrollment. Call +1 (800) 555-7866 or submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com — identity verification is required before MFA is reset.

I got an Okta Verify push notification I did not request — what should I do?

Deny the request immediately and do not approve it. This may indicate that someone is attempting to use your credentials. Change your Okta password right away at https://summit.okta.com and report the incident to soc@summitit.com. This is a potential credential compromise and should be reported even if you think it was a mistake.

How do I set up Okta Verify on a new phone?

Before wiping or replacing your old phone, log in to your Okta dashboard, go to Settings > Extra Verification, and remove the old device. Then enroll the new device by clicking Set up next to Okta Verify and scanning the QR code. If you have already lost access to your old device, contact the Service Desk to perform the enrollment reset for you.

What is 1Password and should I use it instead of my browser's built-in password manager?

1Password Business is Summit IT's approved password manager — use it for all work credentials. Browser-built-in password managers (Chrome, Edge, Safari) are not approved for storing work passwords, as they do not meet Summit IT's security standards. Store all work-related passwords, secure notes, and multi-factor recovery codes in 1Password.

Can I use the same password for Okta that I use for personal accounts?

No. Work passwords must be unique and must not be reused across personal and professional accounts. Using 1Password to generate and store strong, unique passwords for every account is the recommended approach and makes this easy to manage.

Why do I keep getting signed out of applications even though I remember my password?

Okta sessions expire after a period of inactivity (typically 8 hours for browser sessions). You can re-authenticate quickly through your Okta dashboard at https://summit.okta.com and approve the MFA push. If you are being signed out unusually frequently, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com as there may be an SSO configuration issue.

I can log in to Okta but a specific application is not working — what do I do?

This typically indicates an app-level permission or provisioning issue rather than a password problem. Submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com specifying the application name and the exact error message. Include a screenshot if possible. The Service Desk will investigate your application access and provisioning.

How does Single Sign-On (SSO) work at Summit IT?

Okta SSO allows you to authenticate once via https://summit.okta.com and then launch Microsoft 365, Slack, Zoom, SummitDesk, 1Password, and other integrated apps without re-entering passwords. Your Okta session token is passed securely to each application. SSO also means that resetting your Okta password automatically protects all connected apps.

What should I do if I think someone else knows my Okta password?

Change your password immediately at https://summit.okta.com and notify the Security team at soc@summitit.com. Review your recent Okta login history (Settings > My Profile > Login history) for any unfamiliar access. Do not wait — credential exposure can affect all Okta-connected applications.

Do I need a separate password for Microsoft 365 / Outlook?

No. Microsoft 365 is integrated with Okta SSO. You authenticate through Okta, and that session grants access to Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint, and all other M365 apps. If you are prompted for a Microsoft password separately, contact the Service Desk as this may indicate an SSO misconfiguration.

What happens when my password expires?

Okta will prompt you to change your password the next time you log in after the 90-day expiry window. You cannot skip this step — you must set a new password before accessing any applications. Follow the on-screen prompts or go to https://summit.okta.com to complete the change. Set a calendar reminder a week in advance to avoid being caught off-guard.

I Can't Print

Use this guide to diagnose and resolve printing issues with Summit IT-managed HP and Xerox multifunction printers (MFPs), managed through PaperCut.

Start Here

Summit IT printing is managed through PaperCut, which handles print job routing, authentication, and quota management. Printers are HP and Xerox MFPs located throughout Summit IT offices.

Identify the situation: - Are you trying to print from an office printer or a home printer? (IT supports only office printers.) - Is the printer showing in your list of available printers? - What happens when you try to print? (Error message, job sits in queue, "printer offline" shown, job silently disappears?) - Are you on the corporate network or VPN? (Printing to office printers requires being on-site or connected via GlobalProtect VPN with PaperCut access.)

Step-by-Step Diagnostic Flow

1. Is the printer physically powered on and showing a ready status on its front panel?

If no or the panel shows an error (paper jam, toner low, door open, etc.) → - Clear the paper jam following the on-screen instructions on the printer panel — open all indicated doors and remove any jammed paper gently. - Replace the toner cartridge if the toner-low warning is active (spares are in the supply cabinet near the printer or can be requested via https://help.summitit.com). - Close any open doors securely and wait for the printer to return to "Ready" before retrying.

If yes, printer panel shows "Ready" → Proceed to Step 2.

2. Is the printer listed on your laptop as an available printer?

Windows: Go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners and look for the printer by name.

Mac: Go to System Settings > Printers & Scanners and check the list.

If the printer is not listed → Proceed to Step 3 to add it.

If the printer is listed → Proceed to Step 4.

3. Add the printer to your laptop.

Windows: 1. Open Company Portal and search for "Print" — Intune may have a pre-configured printer package for your office. Install if available. 2. Alternatively, go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners > Add a printer or scanner. Wait for your office printer to appear and click Add device. 3. If the printer does not appear automatically, click The printer that I want isn't listed and enter the printer's hostname or IP address (available from the label on the printer or from the Service Desk).

Mac: 1. Open Self Service and search for the printer — Jamf may have a pre-configured printer profile for your office. Install if available. 2. Alternatively, go to System Settings > Printers & Scanners, click the + button, and select the printer from the list or enter its IP address.

After adding, try printing a test page. Did the print succeed?

If yes → Printer is now installed and working.

If no → Proceed to Step 4.

4. Is the printer showing as "Offline" in your printer list?

Windows: Right-click the printer in Settings > Printers & scanners and select See what's printing. In the menu, go to Printer and check whether "Use Printer Offline" is ticked. If so, uncheck it.

Mac: Open the print queue for the printer (click the printer name in Printers & Scanners) and look for an "offline" status. Click Resume if it is paused.

Did taking it offline resolve the issue?

If yes → Issue was a software-level "offline" flag. Monitor for recurrence.

If no → Proceed to Step 5.

5. Clear the print queue.

A stuck print job can block all subsequent jobs. Clear the queue before sending a new job.

Windows: 1. Open Settings > Printers & scanners, click the printer, and select Open print queue. 2. Right-click each job in the queue and select Cancel. If jobs cannot be cancelled: 3. Stop the Print Spooler: open Services (search in Start), find Print Spooler, right-click it, and select Stop. 4. Delete all files in C:\Windows\System32\spool\PRINTERS\. 5. Restart the Print Spooler service.

Mac: 1. Open Printers & Scanners, click the printer, and click Open Print Queue. 2. Click the X next to each job to cancel it. 3. If a job is stuck, pause the printer first, delete the jobs, then resume.

After clearing the queue, retry printing. Did it work?

If yes → A stuck job was blocking the queue.

If no → Proceed to Step 6.

6. Can you reach the printer over the network?

Windows: Open Command Prompt and type ping <printer-IP-address>. The printer's IP is on the label on the device or available from the Service Desk.

Mac: Open Terminal and type ping -c 4 <printer-IP-address>.

If ping fails (Request timed out / 100% packet loss) → You cannot reach the printer on the network. Proceed to Step 7.

If ping succeeds but printing still fails → The printer driver or PaperCut configuration may be the issue. Proceed to Step 8.

7. Verify network and VPN connectivity.

If you are in the office: Ensure your laptop is connected to Summit-Corp Wi-Fi or the wired network via your docking station. If connected to Summit-Guest (guest Wi-Fi), office printers are not accessible — connect to Summit-Corp instead.

If you are remote: Ensure GlobalProtect VPN is connected. Printing to office printers from off-site requires VPN. If VPN is connected and ping still fails, the printer may be down — contact the Service Desk.

After correcting network connectivity, retry printing. Did it work?

If yes → Network was the issue.

If no → Proceed to Step 8.

8. Is PaperCut prompting you for authentication or showing a quota error?

PaperCut requires you to authenticate with your firstname.lastname@summitit.com credentials when prompted.

If you see a login prompt: Enter your Okta credentials. If it does not accept them, your PaperCut account may not be provisioned — submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com.

If you see a quota or balance error: Your print quota may be exhausted. Contact the Service Desk to review or increase your quota.

If PaperCut is not appearing at all and jobs vanish silently: The PaperCut client may not be installed. Check Company Portal (Windows) or Self Service (Mac) for the PaperCut print client and install it.

9. Reinstall the printer driver.

Windows: Go to Settings > Printers & scanners, select the printer, click Remove device. Then re-add the printer following Step 3. If a specific driver is needed, the Service Desk can push it via Intune.

Mac: Go to System Settings > Printers & Scanners, select the printer, click the – button to remove it. Re-add it following Step 3. Jamf can push the correct PPD/driver if needed.

Quick Checklist

Before calling the Service Desk, confirm you have tried:

  • [ ] Verified the printer's front panel shows "Ready" (no paper jam, toner, or door errors)
  • [ ] Confirmed the printer is installed on your laptop (listed in printers)
  • [ ] Confirmed the printer is not showing as "Offline"
  • [ ] Cleared the print queue (including stopping/restarting Print Spooler on Windows)
  • [ ] Pinged the printer to verify network reachability
  • [ ] Confirmed you are on Summit-Corp Wi-Fi (in office) or GlobalProtect VPN (remote), not Summit-Guest
  • [ ] Authenticated in PaperCut with Okta credentials and checked quota
  • [ ] Tried removing and re-adding the printer

My Computer Won't Turn On

Use this guide to diagnose and resolve a laptop that shows no signs of life or fails to boot. Covers Dell Latitude 7440 (Windows) and MacBook Pro 14" (Mac).

Start Here

Before working through the steps below, collect the following information: - Your laptop's asset tag (on the bottom of the device: SUM-LT-##### for Dell, SUM-MB-##### for Mac) - Whether the laptop was previously working normally or this is a new device - Whether you are in an office or working remotely

Press the power button once. Observe what happens (lights, fan spin, screen activity) before proceeding.

Step-by-Step Diagnostic Flow

1. Is the power adapter connected to the laptop?

If no → Connect the OEM power adapter that came with your laptop and wait 2 minutes, then press the power button.

If yes → Proceed to Step 2.

2. Is the power adapter plugged into a working wall outlet or power strip?

If no → Plug the adapter directly into a different wall outlet known to be working. Avoid using power strips if possible — plug directly into the wall.

If yes, but via a power strip or UPS → Plug directly into the wall outlet and wait 2 minutes, then press the power button.

If yes, directly into the wall → Proceed to Step 3.

3. Are you using a docking station?

If yes → Disconnect the dock and connect the power adapter directly to the laptop. Press the power button. Docks can sometimes fail to deliver power correctly. If the laptop boots without the dock, the dock may be faulty — submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com for a dock replacement.

If no → Proceed to Step 4.

4. Is there any indication of power at all? (LED indicator, fan noise, keyboard backlight)

If yes (LED on, fan spins, but no display) → The laptop is receiving power but may have a display issue. Proceed to Step 5.

If no (completely dead — no lights, no sound, nothing) → Proceed to Step 6.

5. Does the laptop produce a display if you connect an external monitor?

If yes → The laptop's built-in screen may be faulty. The laptop is otherwise functional. Submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com (P3 – Medium) for a screen repair or replacement and continue working with the external monitor in the meantime.

If no → The laptop is not booting into the OS. Proceed to Step 6.

6. Perform a forced power cycle.

Dell Latitude: 1. Hold the power button for 30 seconds to discharge residual power. 2. Connect the power adapter. 3. Press the power button normally.

MacBook Pro: 1. Hold the power button for 10 seconds until the laptop shuts down. 2. Wait 5 seconds. 3. Press the power button normally.

Did the laptop power on after this step?

If yes → Monitor the laptop for recurrence. If it happens again within the same week, submit a ticket at https://help.summitit.com (P3 – Medium) for a diagnostic checkup.

If no → Proceed to Step 7.

7. Is the battery completely drained (Dell Latitude only)?

Connect the Dell power adapter and leave the laptop plugged in for 30 minutes without pressing the power button. The battery may be fully discharged. After 30 minutes, press the power button.

If yes, it powers on → Battery was depleted. Allow to charge fully before unplugging again.

If no, still no power → Proceed to Step 8.

8. Is the laptop showing a BIOS/firmware error or blinking LED pattern?

Some Dell Latitude models display an LED blink code to indicate hardware failure (e.g., 3 amber blinks = memory error). Count the blink pattern and note it for the Service Desk.

If a blink code is present → This indicates a hardware component failure. Contact the Service Desk immediately — see "When to Contact the Service Desk" below.

If no pattern → Proceed to Step 9.

9. Is this a brand-new laptop that has never been set up?

If yes → New devices are shipped with a minimal battery charge. Connect the power adapter, wait 5 minutes, then press the power button. If there is still no response, this is a dead-on-arrival (DOA) unit — contact the Service Desk.

If no → The laptop has failed to respond to all standard recovery steps. Contact the Service Desk.

Quick Checklist

Before calling the Service Desk, confirm you have tried:

  • [ ] Connecting a known-working power adapter directly to the wall (not via dock or power strip)
  • [ ] Performing a 30-second (Dell) or 10-second (Mac) forced power cycle
  • [ ] Connecting an external monitor to check for display-only failure (Dell)
  • [ ] Letting the laptop charge for 30 minutes without pressing power
  • [ ] Noting any LED blink codes or unusual sounds
  • [ ] Checking your asset tag number (SUM-LT-##### or SUM-MB-#####)

When to Contact the Service Desk

Scenario Priority

Brand-new laptop that will not power on (dead on arrival) P2 – High
